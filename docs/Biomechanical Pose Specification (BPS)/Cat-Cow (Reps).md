# Cat-Cow (Reps) — Biomechanical Pose Specification

## 1. Exercise Overview
Cat-Cow is a quadruped spinal mobility flow performed on the hands and knees. It cycles between two end-range spinal postures: Cat (full spinal flexion — rounding the back, tucking the chin and tail) and Cow (full spinal extension — arching the back, lifting the chest, head, and tail). Performed as flowing repetitions, it mobilizes the entire vertebral column, particularly the thoracic and lumbar regions, and coordinates breath with spinal motion. The canonical reference poses are the Cat (flexed) end range and the Cow (extended) end range.

Populations: warm-ups, mobility work, yoga and Pilates, low-back health routines. No equipment required.

## 2. Pose Purpose
- Mobilize the full spine through its available flexion and extension ranges.
- Improve thoracic and lumbar segmental articulation and intervertebral motion.
- Coordinate breath with spinal movement (exhale into Cat, inhale into Cow).
- Restore awareness of neutral spine between the two end ranges.

## 3. Whole-Body Alignment
- Base: quadruped (hands under shoulders, knees under hips), a stable four-point base.
- Cat: the spine rounds upward into full flexion; the pelvis posteriorly tilts (tail tuck); the head drops (chin to chest); the shoulders and hips stay over the wrists and knees.
- Cow: the spine arches into full extension; the pelvis anteriorly tilts (tail lifts); the chest and head lift; the abdomen gently sinks toward the floor.
- The flow moves smoothly between the two; at neutral midpoint the spine is in its natural lordosis/kyphosis.
- Movement is symmetric about the midline: no rotation, no lateral flexion of the trunk.

## 4. Head
- Cat: cervical spine flexes fully — chin tucks toward the chest, the crown of the head drops, gaze toward the navel.
- Cow: cervical spine extends — the head lifts, gaze is forward/up (not cranked), the chin gently leads the chest lift.
- The head leads the spinal wave at each end but does not over-extend the neck in Cow (no sharp craning).

## 5. Spine
- Cat (flexion end range): the entire spine rounds — thoracic and lumbar flexion maximal, the back becomes a convex "cat" arch; the vertebral column lifts toward the ceiling.
- Cow (extension end range): the entire spine arches — thoracic and lumbar extension maximal, the back becomes a concave "sway" arch; the abdomen drops.
- The motion is a sequential wave from the pelvis/coccyx through the lumbar, thoracic, and cervical segments.
- No rotation and no lateral flexion at any point; the spine moves purely in the sagittal plane.
- Neutral (midpoint) is the natural resting curve; reps flow Cat → neutral → Cow → neutral.
- Consensus: ranges are full but controlled; end-range is comfortable, not forced or ballistic.

## 6. Upper Body
- Hands flat under shoulders, fingers spread, wrists neutral; elbows extended (not locked).
- Scapulae move with the spine: in Cat they protract slightly and round; in Cow they retract and widen as the chest opens.
- The shoulders stay over the wrists in both end ranges; weight is even through both hands.
- In Cow, the chest lifts and the shoulder girdle opens (no shrug); in Cat, the shoulder blades spread apart as the upper back domes.

## 7. Lower Body
- Knees under hips, shins on the floor; the thighs stay vertical in both end ranges.
- The pelvis drives the motion: in Cat it posteriorly tilts (tailbone tucks under); in Cow it anteriorly tilts (tailbone lifts).
- Hip joints stay at ~90° flexion; the movement originates from the lumbar spine and pelvis, not from shifting the knee position.
- Weight is even through both knees; the thighs do not splay.

## 8. Contacts
- Four-point base throughout: both hands (palm/carpal arch) and both knees (patella/shin on a padded surface) remain in contact with the floor.
- Hand contact: weight through the carpal arch, wrists neutral, fingers spread.
- Knee contact: centered on the shins, not rolled inward or outward.
- The floor supports the four points while the spine articulates above them; no contact is broken during the flow.

## 9. Range of Motion
- Spinal flexion (Cat): maximal comfortable thoracic + lumbar flexion; the back rounds fully.
- Spinal extension (Cow): maximal comfortable thoracic + lumbar extension; the back arches fully.
- Pelvis: posterior tilt in Cat, anterior tilt in Cow — the full tilt range between the two.
- Cervical spine: full flexion in Cat (~chin-to-chest), full extension in Cow (~looking forward/up).
- Shoulder: stays ~90° flexion over the wrist; scapular protraction/retraction accompanies the spine.
- Hip: ~90° flexion maintained; knee ~90° (shins grounded). The limb joints are stable; ROM is in the spine.

## 10. Muscle Intent
- Cat: erector spinae lengthen (eccentric) as the spine flexes; rectus abdominis and internal/external obliques concentrically round the trunk; deep pelvic tilt via the gluteals and hamstrings (posterior tilt).
- Cow: erector spinae concentrically extend the spine; hip flexors and lumbar extensors lift the pelvis into anterior tilt; the abdominal wall lengthens.
- Scapular stabilizers (serratus anterior, trapezius) coordinate the shoulder girdle with the spinal wave.
- Transversus abdominis: gently controls the pressure and the neutral transitions.
- Neck flexors/extensors: follow the spinal wave at each end.

## 12. Common Mistakes
- Moving only the lumbar spine and stiffening the thoracic/ cervical (failing to articulate the whole column).
- Cranking the neck excessively in Cow (looking sharply up) rather than a flowing cervical extension.
- Letting the shoulders shift forward/back off the wrists.
- Collapsing the wrists or rolling onto the inner/outer knees.
- Adding rotation or side-bend (the motion should be sagittal only).
- Forcing end-range beyond comfort (ballistic or compressive).
- Holding the breath instead of syncing with the wave.
- Letting the abdomen drop uncontrolled in Cow without spinal extension.
- Rushing the tempo so the neutral midpoint is skipped.

## 13. Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] Performed from a stable four-point base (hands under shoulders, knees under hips).
- [ ] Cat end range: full spinal flexion with pelvic posterior tilt and chin tuck.
- [ ] Cow end range: full spinal extension with pelvic anterior tilt and chest/head lift.
- [ ] Motion is a symmetric sagittal spinal wave with no rotation or lateral flexion.
- [ ] Breath is coordinated (exhale Cat, inhale Cow) and tempo controlled.
- [ ] Neutral spine is the clean midpoint between repetitions.

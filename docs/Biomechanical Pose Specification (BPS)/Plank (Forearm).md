# Plank (Forearm) — Biomechanical Pose Specification

## 1. Exercise Overview

- **Movement goal:** A static, prone, closed-chain trunk hold supported specifically on the forearms (elbows under the shoulders) and the balls of the feet, with the body held as a single rigid line from shoulders to ankles and no movement beyond isometric bracing.
- **Training goal:** Develop trunk and scapular stability and isometric core endurance with the forearms as the front support — a common, joint-friendly plank variation that reduces wrist load versus the straight-arm version.
- **Intended muscles:** Rectus abdominis, transverse abdominis, internal/external obliques, erector spinae (isometric neutral), gluteus maximus, quadriceps, serratus anterior, trapezius (scapular setting), anterior deltoid (minor).
- **Common execution style:** From a prone position, the forearms are placed on the floor with the elbows directly under the shoulders, the toes tucked, and the body lifted into a straight line and held, breathing steadily, trunk braced, pelvis level.

## 2. Pose Purpose

- **Why it exists:** The forearm plank is the most common plank variant; it trains neutral-spine isometric stability with the forearms as the base, removing wrist extension demand and emphasizing the trunk and scapular setting.
- **Canonical sub-poses:**
  - **Palms-down forearm plank** — forearms flat, palms on the floor.
  - **Fists forearm plank** — forearms on fists, knuckles down.
  - The hold is static; entry/exit is the only transition.
- **Scapular strategy:** Scapulae protracted (serratus anterior) and depressed, flat against the rib cage; the shoulder girdle is set, not collapsed or shrugged.

## 3. Whole-Body Alignment

- **Center of mass:** Mid-torso, projected vertically between the forearm base and the toe contacts; held steady.
- **Balance:** Stable, bilateral, symmetrical.
- **Symmetry:** Mirror-symmetric; equal load both forearms and both feet.
- **Posture:** Neutral spine; a single straight line from acromion through greater trochanter to lateral malleolus.
- **Visual appearance:** A rigid board held off the floor on the forearms and toes; one straight line shoulders to ankles; no hip sag, no butt-up.

## 4. Head

- **Neck:** Neutral, continuation of the spine.
- **Gaze:** At the floor a short distance ahead.
- **Chin:** Slightly tucked.
- **Cervical alignment:** Smooth single curve.

## 5. Spine

- **Lumbar:** Neutral (normal lordosis), braced, not rounded (sag) or arched (hyperextended).
- **Thoracic:** Neutral; flat upper back, scapulae flush.
- **Rib cage:** Closed/down; no flaring.
- **Pelvis:** Neutral and level; no anterior or posterior tilt, no rotation.
- **Summary state:** Neutral spine held isometrically for the hold.

## 6. Upper Body

- **Shoulders (glenohumeral):** The upper arm is vertical from the elbow (under the shoulder) to the shoulder; the humerus is loaded axially through the forearm.
- **Scapula:** Protracted (serratus), depressed, flat; no winging, no shrug.
- **Clavicles:** Level, symmetric.
- **Elbows:** Flexed ~90°, directly under the shoulders; the upper arms vertical.
- **Forearms:** Parallel, flat on the floor, perpendicular to the torso; palms down or fists.
- **Wrists:** Neutral (weight borne through the forearm/palm or knuckles, not wrist extension).
- **Hands:** Flat (palms down) or fists (knuckles down), fingers forward or relaxed.
- **Expected visual appearance:** Forearms parallel and flat, elbows under shoulders; shoulders broad and stable; upper back flat.

## 7. Lower Body

- **Hip:** Extended/neutral; extensors (gluteals, hamstrings) hold the pelvis level.
- **Femur:** In line with the trunk; thighs parallel to floor.
- **Knee:** Extended, straight, held by quadriceps.
- **Ankle:** Plantarflexed; weight on balls of feet (toe contact), heels lifted.
- **Foot:** Toe/ball contact; arches lifted.
- **Toe direction:** Pointed straight back; no rotation.
- **Weight distribution:** Roughly 60–70% on the forearms, 30–40% on the toes; symmetric.

## 8. Contacts

- **Forearm contact (×2):** Forearms flat (palms down or fists) under the shoulders; even pressure; stable front support. Visually: forearms parallel, elbows under shoulders.
- **Toe contact (×2):** Balls of feet; light relative to forearms; stable rear support. Visually: heels lifted, toes back.

## 9. Range of Motion

- **Expected ROM:** Minimal — an isometric hold. Only steady breathing micro-motion is acceptable; the spine and limb angles are held constant.
- **Acceptable variation:** Slight controlled breathing movement; maintained neutral alignment for the hold.
- **Unacceptable variation:** Any sag of the lumbar/pelvis, butt-up, shoulder elevation, or loss of the straight line.

## 10. Muscle Intent

- **Visibly engaged:** Abdominal wall (braced, no sag), gluteus maximus (pelvis level), quadriceps (knees straight), serratus anterior (flat scapulae), trapezius (shoulder setting), erector spinae (neutral hold).

## 11. Visual Checkpoints

- [ ] Body one straight line shoulder–hip–ankle.
- [ ] Head neutral, gaze at floor ahead, chin tucked.
- [ ] Neutral spine; pelvis level (no sag, no butt-up).
- [ ] Rib cage closed.
- [ ] Scapulae flat, depressed, protracted, no winging/shrug.
- [ ] Elbows directly under shoulders, flexed ~90°.
- [ ] Forearms parallel and flat on the floor (palms down or fists).
- [ ] Wrists neutral (no excessive extension).
- [ ] Knees straight, weight on balls of feet, heels up.
- [ ] Feet symmetric, toes back, no rotation.
- [ ] Symmetric load left/right.
- [ ] No torso rotation.
- [ ] Trunk rigid and motionless (save breathing).
- [ ] Shoulders set, not hunched.
- [ ] Neck long.
- [ ] Glutes engaged (pelvis level).
- [ ] No excessive lumbar arch.
- [ ] Alignment maintained for the hold.
- [ ] Breathing steady, not breath-held.
- [ ] Hips not higher than shoulders.

## 12. Common Mistakes

- **Sagging hips (lumbar drop):** Abdominal brace fails; pelvis drops, lumbar rounds. Visually a dip at the waist. Wrong — loads the lumbar spine.
- **Butt-up:** Pelvis rises above the line. Visually inverted-V at hips. Wrong — shortens lever, removes trunk work.
- **Shoulder elevation / shrug:** Traps engage, shoulders to ears. Visually hunched top. Wrong — neck strain.
- **Winging scapulae:** Medial borders lift. Wrong — unstable shoulder girdle.
- **Elbows drifting forward of shoulders:** The forearm angle opens; visually the elbows ahead of the shoulder line. Wrong — reduces scapular setting, strains shoulder.
- **Breath-holding:** Visually tense chest. Wrong — reduces endurance.

## 13. Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Straight-line plank maintained for the full hold.
- [ ] Neutral spine/pelvis; no sag, no butt-up.
- [ ] Head neutral, chin tucked, gaze at floor.
- [ ] Scapulae flat/depressed/protracted, no winging/shrug.
- [ ] Elbows under shoulders; forearms parallel/flat; wrists neutral.
- [ ] Knees straight, weight on balls of feet.
- [ ] Symmetric, no rotation, balanced load.
- [ ] Steady breathing; trunk motionless save breathing.

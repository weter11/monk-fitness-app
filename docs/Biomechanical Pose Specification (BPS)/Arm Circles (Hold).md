# Arm Circles (Hold)

## 1. Exercise Overview
Arm Circles are a standing shoulder-mobility and warm-up exercise in which the arms are extended out to the sides at shoulder height and circled in a controlled manner (small or large diameters), or held isometrically in the abducted "T" position. The canonical position is the arms abducted to 90° (a "T") with the palms facing down, the trunk stable. The exercise promotes controlled shoulder and scapular motion and warms the deltoids and rotator cuff. It may be performed as continuous circling reps or as a static T-hold.

## 2. Pose Purpose
- Warm and mobilize the shoulder girdle (glenohumeral joint and scapulothoracic region).
- Improve shoulder abduction control and scapular stability at 90°.
- Increase blood flow to the deltoids, rotator cuff, and upper back as a warm-up.
- Train trunk stillness while the arms move (core bracing).
- Provide a low-load shoulder-health and coordination drill.

## 3. Whole-Body Alignment
- Standing tall, feet hip-width, knees soft, trunk upright and braced.
- Arms abducted to ~90° (the "T"), palms down for the canonical hold; for circling they move through the arc.
- The trunk stays still — the circles come from the shoulders, not by swaying the torso.
- The motion is smooth and controlled; the neck stays long.
- Breathing is even through the reps/hold.

## 4. Head
- Head is in neutral, upright alignment with the spine.
- The cervical spine is tall; the chin is level.
- The head does not tilt or rotate to follow the arms — it stays centered.

## 5. Spine
- **Whole spine:** neutral and upright (natural curves), lightly braced.
- No spinal flexion, extension, rotation, or lateral lean during the circles.
- The thoracic stays long and slightly open; the lumbar stays neutral.

## 6. Upper Body
- Canonical hold: arms abducted 90°, elbows extended, palms down, shoulders depressed (down, not shrugged).
- Circling: the arms sweep forward/up/back/down (or reverse) in a continuous arc while staying roughly at shoulder height at the sides; the scapulae glide with the arm (upward rotation as the arm rises, downward as it lowers).
- The shoulders stay down and stable — the upper traps do not elevate.
- The chest stays open; the movement is from the shoulders, not a torso swing.

## 7. Lower Body
- Feet hip-width, knees soft, weight evenly distributed.
- The legs are quiet and grounding; they do not drive or sway.
- Hips neutral, pelvis level.
- The lower body is a still base while the arms move.

## 8. Contacts
- Feet on the floor (stable stance).
- No hand or other body contact; the exercise is freestanding.
- (If a prop/band is used for resistance it is held, but the canonical version is unloaded.)

## 9. Range of Motion
- Shoulder abduction: centered on 90° for the hold; for circles the arm sweeps through a forward/up/back/down arc (often ~the scapular plane, not a strict frontal-plane circle).
- Scapular motion: upward rotation as the arm rises, return as it lowers.
- Elbow: extended (straight) throughout.
- Circles may be small (controlled) or large; the canonical hold is static at 90° abduction. Reps or holds of 10–60 s.

## 10. Muscle Intent
- Deltoids (anterior/middle/posterior depending on phase): move and hold the arm at 90°.
- Rotator cuff (supraspinatus, infraspinatus, teres minor, subscapularis): stabilize the glenohumeral joint through the arc.
- Trapezius (upper/middle/lower) and serratus anterior: control scapular glide and keep the shoulders down.
- Core/erectors: keep the trunk upright and still.
- Rhomboids: help set the scapulae.

## 11. Visual Checkpoints
- [ ] Stance is tall, trunk upright, knees soft, weight even.
- [ ] Arms are abducted to ~90° in the canonical T (palms down).
- [ ] Shoulders are depressed, not shrugged toward the ears.
- [ ] The elbows are extended (straight) in the hold.
- [ ] During circles the arms move in a smooth, controlled arc.
- [ ] The trunk does not sway, lean, or rotate.
- [ ] The head stays neutral and centered, not tilting to follow the arms.
- [ ] The chest is open, not collapsed.
- [ ] Both arms move symmetrically.
- [ ] The neck is long, no upper-trap bulging.
- [ ] Breathing is even through the hold/reps.
- [ ] The pelvis stays level, hips neutral.
- [ ] The movement is controlled, not flailing.
- [ ] The low back does not arch.
- [ ] The circle diameter is appropriate (small-to-large, not forced).

## 12. Common Mistakes
- Shrugging the shoulders up toward the ears.
- Swaying or rotating the torso to "help" the circles.
- Letting the elbows bend (losing the straight-arm T).
- Tilting the head to follow the moving arms.
- Rounding the upper back / collapsing the chest.
- Over-arching the low back.
- Circling too fast or with too large a diameter (loss of control).

## 13. Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] The arms hold or sweep at ~90° abduction with straight elbows and depressed shoulders (canonical T).
- [ ] The trunk stays upright and still with the head neutral and centered.
- [ ] Both arms move symmetrically with controlled tempo and even breathing.
- [ ] The chest stays open and the scapulae glide without upper-trap elevation.
- [ ] No torso sway, head tilt, or low-back-arching compensations are present.

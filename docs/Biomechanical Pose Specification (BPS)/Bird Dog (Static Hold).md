# Bird Dog (Static Hold) — Biomechanical Pose Specification

## 1. Exercise Overview
The Bird Dog is a quadruped, contralateral core-stabilization exercise performed on the hands and knees. From the tabletop base, one arm is extended forward and the opposite (contralateral) leg is extended backward, and the position is held isometrically. The defining feature of the hold is that the trunk — pelvis and spine — does not move: it remains level, neutral, and still. The exercise trains the deep trunk stabilizers (multifidus, transversus abdominis, erector spinae) and the gluteal and shoulder stabilizers in a way that resists rotation and tilt under an asymmetric load.

Populations: beginners through advanced; common in rehabilitation, low-back-prevention programs, and general core conditioning. No equipment required.

## 2. Pose Purpose
- Train anti-rotation and anti-extension of the lumbar spine under contralateral limb loading.
- Develop coordinated recruitment of the deep posterior chain (erector spinae, gluteus maximus/medius) with the contralateral shoulder stabilizers (rotator cuff, serratus anterior).
- Teach the pelvis to remain level (no anterior/posterior tilt, no lateral tilt, no rotation) while one arm and the opposite leg are in flight.
- Build proprioceptive control of the trunk in a closed-chain, low-load posture.

## 3. Whole-Body Alignment
- Base position: quadruped (hands and knees), shoulders stacked over wrists, hips stacked over knees.
- From the base, the right arm extends forward and the left leg extends backward (or the mirrored left arm / right leg).
- Head, spine, and pelvis form one neutral, horizontal segment — the trunk is a rigid beam from crown to sacrum.
- The extended arm, trunk, and extended leg should approximate a single straight line parallel to the floor (a "tabletop plus reach" silhouette) when viewed from the side.
- No segment of the trunk dips, rotates, or shifts toward the reaching limbs.

## 4. Head
- Head is a neutral continuation of the cervical spine: gaze is directed down and slightly forward toward the floor, chin gently tucked (no cervical extension, no full face-down drop).
- The cervical spine stays in its natural slight lordosis; the head does not lead the reach or hang heavily.
- Ears stay aligned over the supporting (non-reaching) shoulder; no side bend or rotation of the neck.

## 5. Spine
- Spine is neutral: a natural, gentle lumbar lordosis and thoracic kyphosis are preserved — the back is NOT flattened and NOT arched.
- No rotation of any spinal segment (the shoulders/hips stay square to the floor).
- No lateral flexion (the trunk does not side-bend toward either reaching limb).
- No sagging into lumbar extension and no rounding into flexion during the hold.
- The pelvis is level (neutral tilt): the anterior superior iliac spines (ASIS) remain horizontal; one hip does not hike or drop relative to the other.

## 6. Upper Body
- Supporting (grounded) arm: shoulder directly over the wrist; elbow extended but not locked; scapula depressed and slightly protracted, firmly set (no winging).
- Reaching arm: extended straight forward along the floor line, thumb up or neutral; shoulder in neutral flexion (~180° from the torso, parallel to floor); scapula gently retracted toward the spine without shrugging; the shoulder does not elevate toward the ear.
- The two shoulders remain level (no rotation of the thoracic cage).

## 7. Lower Body
- Supporting (grounded) leg: hip and knee at ~90°, shin vertical, foot relaxed or dorsiflexed; the thigh is loaded as a stable pillar.
- Reaching leg: extended straight back, hip in neutral extension (thigh in line with the pelvis, parallel to floor), knee extended, foot relaxed or pointed; the leg reaches from the hip, not swung upward.
- The reaching hip does not hike (no pelvic lateral tilt) and does not externally rotate the thigh outward.
- The supporting knee stays centered over the hip; weight is distributed evenly across the supporting hand and knee.

## 8. Contacts
- Four-point base reduced to a three-point base during the hold: one hand and the opposite knee remain in contact with the floor; the reaching hand and reaching knee are off the floor.
- Hand contact: palm flat, fingers spread, weight through the carpal arch (not the heel of the hand); wrist neutral.
- Knee contact: the patella/shin region rests on a padded surface; weight centered, not rolled onto the inner or outer knee.
- The floor supports the three grounded points; the trunk floats above it as a rigid, level beam.

## 9. Range of Motion
- Shoulder flexion of the reaching arm: to ~180° (arm parallel to floor), not beyond into scapular elevation.
- Hip extension of the reaching leg: to neutral extension where the thigh is collinear with the pelvis (~0° additional extension beyond the tabletop line), not hiked upward.
- Knee extension of the reaching leg: full (~0°), from the flexed 90° base.
- Spine: held at neutral; ROM of movement is essentially zero — the goal is NO motion (isometric).
- Pelvis: held at neutral; rotation/tilt ROM is zero during the hold.
- Neck: mild flexion (~20–30°) to maintain neutral cervical alignment; no end-range loading.

## 10. Muscle Intent
- Deep core: transversus abdominis and internal obliques brace the abdomen (abdominal drawing-in + gentle hollow); multifidus co-contracts segmentally along the spine.
- Erector spinae: isometric holding of neutral spinal extension against gravity.
- Gluteus maximus and gluteus medius (reaching leg): hip extension and pelvic stabilization against contralateral drop.
- Gluteus medius (supporting hip): prevents pelvic drop on the unsupported side.
- Shoulder stabilizers (reaching side): rotator cuff, serratus anterior, middle/lower trapezius maintain a steady, level reach.
- Quadriceps (supporting leg): maintains the 90° knee stance.

## 11. Visual Checkpoints
- [ ] Hands are directly under shoulders; knees directly under hips.
- [ ] Reaching arm is straight and parallel to the floor.
- [ ] Reaching leg is straight and parallel to the floor.
- [ ] Arm, trunk, and leg form one horizontal line from the side.
- [ ] Pelvis is level (no hip hike or drop).
- [ ] Low back is neutral — not arched, not rounded.
- [ ] Shoulders are square to the floor (no rotation of the chest).
- [ ] Hips are square to the floor (no twist of the pelvis).
- [ ] Chin is gently tucked; gaze is down-forward.
- [ ] Supporting scapula is flat (not winged).
- [ ] Reaching shoulder is not shrugged toward the ear.
- [ ] The trunk does not sag or rotate toward the reaching limbs.
- [ ] Weight is evenly shared between supporting hand and knee.
- [ ] Wrist of supporting hand is neutral, not bent.
- [ ] Breathing is steady (no breath-holding).
- [ ] The position is held still (no tremor-driven trunk sway).
- [ ] The non-reaching hand and knee stay planted.
- [ ] Toes of the reaching foot are relaxed or lightly pointed, not curled.
- [ ] Neck alignment matches the neutral spine (no crane forward).
- [ ] Both ASIS remain horizontal when viewed from front.

## 12. Common Mistakes
- Arching the low back (lumbar extension) to "lift" the reaching leg higher — the leg should reach from the hip, not by hinging the spine.
- Hiking the reaching-side hip (pelvic lateral tilt) to gain height.
- Rotating the pelvis/trunk toward the reaching limbs (losing the square, anti-rotation goal).
- Shrugging the reaching shoulder toward the ear (upper-trapezius dominance instead of scapular setting).
- Dropping the head and cervical spine into flexion, or craning it forward/up.
- Letting the supporting knee drift inward or the supporting wrist collapse.
- Holding the breath (Valsalva) instead of maintaining calm respiration.
- Reaching the arm/leg so high that the trunk line breaks.

## 13. Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] One arm extended forward and the opposite leg extended backward, both parallel to the floor.
- [ ] Spine held in neutral lordosis/kyphosis with zero rotation and zero lateral flexion.
- [ ] Pelvis level and neutral (no tilt, no rotation) for the duration of the hold.
- [ ] Supporting hand and opposite knee remain the only grounded contacts; trunk stays level.
- [ ] Shoulders and hips remain square to the floor (no twist of the trunk).
- [ ] Head neutral, gaze down-forward, neck aligned with the spine.
- [ ] Position maintained isometrically without trunk movement for the prescribed hold time.

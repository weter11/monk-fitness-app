# Scapular Retraction (Hold)

## 1. Exercise Overview
Scapular Retraction is a standing postural hold that isolates the action of squeezing the shoulder blades together (retraction) and downward (depression). The arms may hang relaxed at the sides or be slightly bent; the emphasis is purely on the scapulothoracic movement, not on lifting weight. It is a foundational posture and shoulder-health drill, often used as a warm-up or as a corrective cue. The canonical held position is the "pinch" — shoulder blades drawn together and down, chest open, with the arms quiet.

## 2. Pose Purpose
- Strengthen and teach scapular retraction (rhomboids, middle trapezius) and depression (lower trapezius).
- Improve upright posture and open the chest.
- Train scapular control independent of the arms (the shoulders do not shrug).
- Provide a reference "set" position for pressing, rowing, and postural tasks.
- Reduce upper-trapezius dominance and neck/shoulder tension.

## 3. Whole-Body Alignment
- Standing tall, feet hip-width, knees soft, trunk upright.
- The chest is open and the shoulders are set down and back.
- The scapulae pinch together and slide downward; the motion is at the shoulder girdle, not the arms.
- The trunk stays still; the neck stays long.
- The hold is static; breathing remains easy.

## 4. Head
- Head is in neutral, upright alignment — not poking forward (no "forward head").
- The cervical spine is tall; the chin is level, not jutting.
- The ears align over the shoulders (good postural stack).

## 5. Spine
- **Whole spine:** neutral and upright, with a slight natural thoracic extension (open chest) and natural lumbar lordosis.
- No rounding of the upper back (which would undo the retraction) and no excessive arching.
- The trunk is a quiet, braced base.

## 6. Upper Body
- Shoulder blades retract (squeeze toward the spine) and depress (pull down toward the hips).
- Arms hang relaxed or are slightly bent at the elbows; they are NOT lifted to create the pinch.
- The shoulders stay down — the upper traps do not elevate; there is a clear gap between the ears and the shoulders.
- The chest opens as the scapulae come together; the pectorals lengthen slightly.
- This is a "set the girdle" hold, not an arm exercise.

## 7. Lower Body
- Feet hip-width, knees soft, weight evenly distributed.
- The legs are quiet; they simply ground the stance.
- Hips neutral, pelvis level.
- No lower-body movement contributes to the hold.

## 8. Contacts
- Feet on the floor (stable stance).
- No hand or other body contact required; the hold is freestanding.
- (Optionally the arms may rest lightly at the sides but do not bear weight.)

## 9. Range of Motion
- Scapular retraction: from resting (scapulae neutral/protracted) to end-range squeeze toward the spine.
- Scapular depression: from neutral to the blades pulled down.
- The combined "pinch and down" is held 5–30 s, repeated.
- The range is small (scapular motion is measured in centimeters) but distinct and controllable.

## 10. Muscle Intent
- Rhomboids (major/minor) and middle trapezius: retract the scapulae (squeeze together).
- Lower trapezius: depress the scapulae (pull down).
- Serratus anterior: stabilizes the scapula against the rib cage (co-contraction).
- Core/erectors: keep the trunk upright.
- Upper trapezius: intentionally quiet (not elevating).

## 11. Visual Checkpoints
- [ ] Stance is tall, trunk upright, knees soft.
- [ ] The shoulder blades are visibly squeezed together.
- [ ] The shoulders are pulled DOWN (not shrugged toward the ears).
- [ ] The chest is open, not collapsed forward.
- [ ] The arms are relaxed, not lifted to fake the pinch.
- [ ] The head is neutral, ears over shoulders, chin level.
- [ ] The upper back is slightly extended (open), not rounded.
- [ ] Both sides pinch symmetrically.
- [ ] The neck is long, no tension bulge at the upper traps.
- [ ] Breathing is easy through the hold.
- [ ] The pelvis is level, hips neutral.
- [ ] The hold is static, not bouncing the blades.
- [ ] The low back is not over-arched.
- [ ] Weight is evenly distributed on both feet.
- [ ] No jaw clenching or breath-holding.

## 12. Common Mistakes
- Shrugging the shoulders up (upper-trap dominance) instead of depressing.
- Rounding the upper back, which prevents true retraction.
- Lifting the arms to fake the pinch (the arms should be quiet).
- Poking the head forward (forward-head posture) while "retracting."
- Over-arching the low back.
- Holding the breath.
- Asymmetric pinch (one blade working more).

## 13. Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] The shoulder blades are retracted and depressed with the chest open and the arms relaxed.
- [ ] The head and spine are in neutral upright alignment, ears over shoulders.
- [ ] The shoulders stay down (no upper-trap elevation) and the pinch is symmetrical.
- [ ] The position is held statically with relaxed breathing and an even stance.
- [ ] No forward-head, rounded-back, or low-back-arching compensations are present.

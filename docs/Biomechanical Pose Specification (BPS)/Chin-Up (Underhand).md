# Chin-Up (Underhand)

## 1. Exercise Overview
The chin-up is a closed-chain vertical pull performed hanging from a fixed horizontal bar with a supinated (underhand, palms-toward-face) grip and a narrow hand placement (approximately 0.9× biacromial shoulder width). Supination places the biceps brachii in a mechanically stronger elbow-flexion position, so the chin-up is generally more biceps-dominant than the pronated pull-up while still heavily involving the latissimus dorsi. The narrower grip lets the elbows track close to the ribs and forward, and the top position tends to include a slight forward torso arc with thoracic extension as the sternum drives up.

## 2. Pose Purpose
This document specifies the biomechanics of a single chin-up repetition, from the extended hang to the top. The supinated, narrow grip makes the biceps the prime elbow flexor and lets the elbows travel close to the body and slightly forward. The chest (sternum) drives upward and the chin clears the bar; at the top there is a natural, slight forward lean of the thorax with mild thoracic extension. As with all vertical pulls, the scapulae initiate the motion (depress + retract) before elbow flexion.

## 3. Whole-Body Alignment
- The body hangs in a vertical line beneath the bar; the narrow supinated grip keeps the upper arms close to the torso.
- The trunk is braced and neutral through the mid-range; near the top a slight forward arc of the thorax and mild thoracic extension is expected and acceptable (it is the natural end-range of a supinated pull).
- A slight, controlled forward leg pendulum is permitted but must not swing the torso.
- The midline stays close to a single sagittal plane; no rotation.

## 4. Head
- Cervical spine neutral to slightly extended relative to the thorax; the chin is not tucked.
- Gaze is forward and slightly upward as the sternum rises; the chin clears the bar at the top.
- The neck is relaxed; no shoulder elevation toward the ears.

## 5. Spine
- Thoracic spine neutral through the pull, reaching mild extension at the top (the chest opens upward toward the bar). Lumbar spine neutral (no excessive arch).
- The pull initiates at the scapulae: from the relaxed, elevated, protracted hang, the lower trapezius and serratus anterior depress the scapulae while the rhomboids and middle trapezius retract them, raising the body a few units with arms essentially straight (active hang).
- Continued pulling is driven by strong elbow flexion (biceps) and shoulder extension/adduction, with the sternum leading up and a slight forward thoracic arc at the top.
- The spine does not flex; the top-end thoracic extension is controlled, not hyper-extended.

## 6. Upper Body
- **Grip:** supinated (underhand, palms toward the face). Hands narrow (~0.9× shoulder width). Full grip.
- **Shoulders (glenohumeral):** humeri hang near-neutral abduction (~10–15°) and track close to the torso. Shoulders extend and adduct as the elbows flex and draw down and forward (close to the ribs).
- **Elbows:** flex with high efficiency (supinated biceps) and track close to the ribs and slightly forward. At the top the elbows are bent ~70–100° and pinned to the sides.
- **Wrists/hands:** fixed on the bar (palms up); wrists in neutral to slight extension.
- **Muscles (primary):** biceps brachii (dominant elbow flexor in supination), brachialis, latissimus dorsi (adduction/extension), pectoralis major (sternal, draws humerus down/forward), rhomboids/middle trapezius (retraction), lower trapezius/serratus anterior (depression), with the biceps anterior emphasis being the key difference from the pull-up.

## 7. Lower Body
- Hips and knees extended or slightly flexed; legs hang with a slight controlled forward pendulum.
- Pelvis neutral to very slightly posteriorly tilted as the thorax extends at the top (to avoid lumbar compensation); glutes gently engaged.
- Feet relaxed, not driving the movement.
- The lower body is a stable, non-swinging mass.

## 8. Contacts
- **Primary (anterior/upper) contact:** both hands fixed on the horizontal bar in a supinated grip, narrow. The hands are the only external anchor; the body is lifted relative to them.
- **Secondary contact:** none. Feet are free.
- **Contact stability:** the grip is secure and static; hands do not slide or regrip.
- **Bottom reference:** full extended hang, arms straight, shoulders depressed (not shrugged), feet hanging free.

## 9. Range of Motion
- **Shoulder girdle:** full depression and retraction at top; full elevation and protraction at bottom.
- **Glenohumeral:** from high shoulder elevation in the hang to near-neutral at the top, humerus close to torso.
- **Elbow:** from ~175–180° (extended) at bottom to ~70–100° of flexion at top (elbows forward/close).
- **Wrist:** neutral to slight extension.
- **Spine/trunk:** neutral through the pull; mild controlled thoracic extension at the top; no rotation.
- **Vertical travel:** chin clears the bar at the top; bottom is the full hang.

## 10. Muscle Intent
- **Initiation:** lower trapezius + serratus anterior depress; rhomboids + middle trapezius retract; body rises with arms straight.
- **Pull:** biceps brachii (supinated, strong) and brachialis flex the elbows; latissimus dorsi adducts/extends the humerus; pectoralis major draws the upper arm down and forward so the sternum leads up; the top includes a slight thoracic extension as the chest opens.
- **Stabilization:** anterior core and glutes brace the trunk; forearm flexors and grip maintain the fixed contact; scapular upward rotators control the path.

## 11. Visual Checkpoints
- [ ] ✓ Hands fixed on the bar, supinated (palms toward face), narrow (~0.9× shoulder width).
- [ ] ✓ Arms fully extended at the bottom; shoulders depressed, not shrugged.
- [ ] ✓ The first motion is the scapulae dropping and squeezing together (body rises a few units, elbows straight).
- [ ] ✓ The elbows flex and track close to the ribs and slightly forward.
- [ ] ✓ The sternum (chest) drives upward toward the bar.
- [ ] ✓ The chin clears the bar at the top.
- [ ] ✓ A slight, controlled forward thoracic arc / mild extension appears at the top (natural for the supinated pull).
- [ ] ✓ The trunk stays braced; no swinging, no excessive lumbar arch.
- [ ] ✓ The head is neutral to slightly extended; neck long, not jutted.
- [ ] ✓ The legs hang with a slight controlled forward pendulum.
- [ ] ✓ Gaze forward/up as the chest rises.
- [ ] ✓ Bottom position is a full hang with depressed shoulders.
- [ ] ✓ No visible upper-trap shrugging at the top.
- [ ] ✓ The path is smooth: scapular set → elbow flex (biceps) → chest to bar.
- [ ] ✓ Descent retraces the same controlled path.

## 12. Common Mistakes
- **Shrugging:** elevating the shoulders instead of depressing them.
- **Elbows flaring:** letting the elbows drift away from the ribs, losing the narrow biceps-friendly path.
- **Swinging/kipping:** using leg/lumbar momentum rather than strict pulling.
- **Over-arching the low back:** excessive lumbar extension at the top instead of containing the arc in the thorax.
- **Chest not leading:** pulling only with the arms, leaving the sternum behind.
- **Partial range:** not reaching a full hang or not clearing the chin.
- **Grip too wide:** widening beyond the narrow intent reduces the biceps advantage and shifts toward a pull-up pattern.

## 13. Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] Hands are fixed on the bar in a supinated (palms-toward-face) grip, narrow (~0.9× shoulder width).
- [ ] At the bottom the arms are fully extended and the shoulders depressed (not shrugged).
- [ ] The pull begins with scapular depression and retraction before elbow flexion.
- [ ] The elbows flex and track close to the ribs and slightly forward.
- [ ] The sternum drives up; the chin clears the bar at the top.
- [ ] A slight, controlled forward thoracic arc/mild extension is present at the top without excessive lumbar arch.
- [ ] The trunk remains braced; no swing.
- [ ] The feet hang free with at most a slight controlled forward pendulum.
- [ ] The descent retraces the same controlled path to a full hang.

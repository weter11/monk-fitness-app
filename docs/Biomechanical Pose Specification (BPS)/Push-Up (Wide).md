# Push-Up (Wide) — Biomechanical Pose Specification

## 1. Exercise Overview

- **Movement goal:** A prone, closed-chain horizontal press identical in principle to the standard push-up, but performed with the hands placed wider than shoulder width, increasing frontal-plane abduction of the humerus at the bottom.
- **Training goal:** Emphasize the pectoralis major (especially the sternal/clavicular fibers stretched across the wider base) and the anterior deltoid, while reducing relative triceps demand compared with a narrow press.
- **Intended muscles:** Pectoralis major (broad recruitment due to wide hand base), anterior deltoid, triceps brachii (lesser role), serratus anterior, rectus abdominis, transverse abdominis, gluteus maximus, quadriceps.
- **Common execution style:** High-plank start with hands placed approximately 1.9× shoulder width apart; elbows flare outward (closer to 70–90° from the torso in the frontal plane) as the body descends; the wider base shortens the effective press lever and shifts stress toward the chest and front shoulder.

## 2. Pose Purpose

- **Why it exists:** The wide push-up is a variation that biases the press toward the pectoral and anterior-deltoid fibers by widening the hand base and increasinghumeral abduction, reducing the triceps' mechanical advantage.
- **Canonical sub-poses:**
  - **Top (plank) position** — arms extended, hands wide, body rigid.
  - **Bottom (depth) position** — elbows flexed and flared outward, chest lowered toward the floor between the wide hands.
  - **Transition** — descent/ascent with the elbows tracking outward rather than tucked.
- **Scapular strategy:** Scapular protraction at the bottom and retraction at the top are preserved, but because the humerus is abducted more, the scapulae sit in a relatively more protracted, depressed position; winging must still be avoided.

## 3. Whole-Body Alignment

- **Center of mass:** Projected vertically between the wide hand base and the toe contacts; it tracks over the hand line during the movement.
- **Balance:** Stable, bilateral, symmetrical; the wider base increases medial-lateral stability.
- **Symmetry:** Mirror-symmetric; equal load across both hands and both feet.
- **Posture:** Neutral spine; a single straight line from acromion through greater trochanter to lateral malleolus.
- **Visual appearance:** A rigid plank with a notably wide hand stance; the upper arms form a broad "V" opening outward from the shoulders at the bottom; the body otherwise reads as one straight board.

## 4. Head

- **Neck:** Neutral continuation of the thoracic spine; not craned or tucked.
- **Gaze:** At the floor a short distance ahead of the hands.
- **Chin:** Slightly tucked; no forward jut.
- **Cervical alignment:** Smooth, single curve with the spine.

## 5. Spine

- **Lumbar:** Neutral (normal lordosis), braced, not rounded or arched.
- **Thoracic:** Neutral to slightly extended; upper back flat.
- **Rib cage:** Closed/down; no flaring.
- **Pelvis:** Neutral and level; no anterior or posterior tilt, no rotation.
- **Summary state:** Neutral spine held isometrically; unchanged between top and bottom.

## 6. Upper Body

- **Shoulders (glenohumeral):** Humeri abducted more widely than standard — roughly 70–90° from the torso in the frontal plane at the bottom — so the elbows track well outward. Greater humeral abduction increases pectoral and anterior-deltoid torque.
- **Scapula:** Depressed and protracted at the bottom, retracting at the top; flat, no winging.
- **Clavicles:** Level, symmetric; no upward rotation/shrug.
- **Elbows:** Flexed at the bottom to ~90°; they point outward (away from the torso) rather than back along the ribs; at the top extended but not aggressively locked.
- **Forearms:** Vertical (wrists under elbows) at the top; may angle slightly outward at the bottom due to the wide hand placement.
- **Wrists:** Extended, straight; load through palm base.
- **Hands:** Flat, fingers forward, placed ~1.9× shoulder width; pressure even across both palms.
- **Expected visual appearance:** Wide hand stance; elbows flared outward forming a broad base; shoulders broad and stable; chest between the hands at the bottom.

## 7. Lower Body

- **Hip:** Extended/neutral; hip extensors hold the pelvis level.
- **Femur:** In line with the trunk; thighs parallel to floor.
- **Knee:** Extended, straight, held by quadriceps.
- **Ankle:** Plantarflexed; weight on balls of feet.
- **Foot:** Toe/ball contact only.
- **Toe direction:** Pointed straight back; no rotation.
- **Weight distribution:** As standard (majority on hands); symmetric.

## 8. Contacts

- **Hand contact (×2):** Flat palms and fingers on floor, placed wide; even pressure both sides; stable wide pivot. Visually: hands far apart, wrists under elbows (or slightly out), fingers spread.
- **Toe contact (×2):** Balls of feet; light relative to hands; stable rear pivot. Visually: heels lifted, toes back.

## 9. Range of Motion

- **Expected ROM:** Elbow flexion top ~180° to bottom ~90°; humerus abducted 70–90° from torso at the bottom; chest descends toward floor between the wide hands.
- **Acceptable variation:** Chest within ~10 cm of floor; elbow angle 70–100°; outward elbow tracking present.
- **Unacceptable variation:** Hands so wide the wrists hurt or shoulders impinge; elbows flared past ~90° causing excessive shoulder stress; chest far from floor (half rep); pelvis sag.

## 10. Muscle Intent

- **Visibly engaged:** Pectoralis major (broad, dominant), anterior deltoid (outer shoulder tension), triceps (present but secondary), serratus anterior (flat scapulae), abdominal wall (braced), gluteals and quadriceps (rigid lower body).

## 11. Visual Checkpoints

- [ ] Body one straight line shoulder–hip–ankle.
- [ ] Hands ~1.9× shoulder width apart.
- [ ] Head neutral, gaze at floor ahead.
- [ ] Neutral spine; pelvis level, not butt-up or sagging.
- [ ] Rib cage closed.
- [ ] Scapulae flat, depressed, no winging.
- [ ] Elbows track outward (70–90° from torso), not pinned to ribs.
- [ ] Chest descends near floor between the wide hands.
- [ ] Elbow angle ~90° at bottom.
- [ ] Knees straight, weight on balls of feet, heels up.
- [ ] Feet symmetric, toes pointed back.
- [ ] Symmetric load left/right.
- [ ] No torso rotation.
- [ ] Wrists straight under elbows.
- [ ] Shoulders not shrugged.
- [ ] Trunk rigid across full ROM.
- [ ] Movement controlled both directions.
- [ ] Hips not dropped below shoulder line.
- [ ] Neck long, not shortened.
- [ ] Wide base stable, no wobble.

## 12. Common Mistakes

- **Excessive width / shoulder impingement:** Hands beyond ~2× shoulder width cause the humeral head to crowd the acromion; visually elbows splayed past 90°. Wrong because it risks shoulder impingement and reduces press efficiency.
- **Sagging hips:** Same as standard; pelvis drops, lumbar rounds. Wrong — lumbar load, lost plank.
- **Elbows flared to 90°+ with wrist pain:** The wide base transfers excessive torque to the wrist and shoulder. Wrong — joint stress.
- **Half reps:** Chest stays high. Wrong — fails ROM.
- **Butt-up:** Inverted-V at hips. Wrong — shortens lever, removes trunk work.
- **Winging scapulae:** Medial borders lift. Wrong — unstable shoulder.

## 13. Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Straight-line plank maintained top and bottom.
- [ ] Hands ~1.9× shoulder width, wrists under elbows.
- [ ] Elbows track outward 70–90° from torso.
- [ ] Chest descends to within ~10 cm of floor at bottom.
- [ ] Neutral spine and pelvis; no sag, no butt-up.
- [ ] Knees straight, weight on balls of feet.
- [ ] Symmetric, no rotation, balanced load.
- [ ] Scapulae flat/depressed, no winging/shrug.
- [ ] Full, controlled ROM both directions.

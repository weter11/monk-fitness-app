# Pull-Up (Standard)

## 1. Exercise Overview
The standard pull-up is a closed-chain vertical pull performed hanging from a fixed horizontal bar. The body is suspended by the hands and lifted toward the bar by shortening the distance between the shoulders and the bar. The grip is overhand (pronated), with the palms facing away from the body and the hands placed roughly shoulder-width apart (approximately 1.35× biacromial shoulder width). It is a foundational upper-body pulling movement trained for relative strength, scapular control, and trunk rigidity.

## 2. Pose Purpose
This document specifies the biomechanics of a single repetition of the standard pull-up, from the bottom dead-hang to the top position with the chin clearing the bar. The pull is initiated by the scapulae (depression and retraction) before the elbows begin to flex; as the shoulder-to-bar reach shortens, the chest leads upward and the chin rises above the bar. The movement is a diagnostic of latissimus dorsi, biceps brachii, and scapular retractor strength, plus anterior core and grip endurance.

## 3. Whole-Body Alignment
- The body hangs in a near-vertical line beneath the bar, with the hands fixed on the bar and the feet free below.
- The trunk is braced: the abdominal wall and glutes are gently engaged so the pelvis does not anteriorly tilt and the low back does not hyperextend (no "hollow" sway beyond neutral, no excessive arch).
- The legs may hang straight or with a slight knee bend; the default alignment is a relaxed, slightly forward leg pendulum that does not swing the torso.
- The midline is maintained: the head, thorax, pelvis, and feet track close to a single sagittal plane.

## 4. Head
- The cervical spine is in neutral extension relative to the thorax—the chin is neither tucked nor jutted.
- Gaze is forward and slightly downward at the bottom; as the chest rises, the chin clears the bar by a small margin at the top.
- The neck is relaxed; there is no shrugging of the upper trapezius toward the ears.

## 5. Spine
- The thoracic spine is held in neutral to slight natural kyphosis; the lumbar spine is in neutral (not arched, not rounded).
- The scapulae govern the first motion: at the bottom they are protracted and elevated (a relaxed hang); the pull begins with scapular depression (downward rotation / lowering of the shoulder girdle away from the ears) and retraction (squeezing the shoulder blades toward the spine).
- This scapular set raises the body a few units while the arms remain essentially straight—the "dead-hang → active-hang" transition.
- From the active-hang, continued pulling is driven by elbow flexion and shoulder extension/adduction, with the chest leading up.
- The spine does not flex or extend excessively during the pull; the trunk travels as a rigid unit.

## 6. Upper Body
- **Grip:** overhand (pronated). Palms face away from the face. Hands are roughly shoulder-width apart (~1.35× biacromial width). The bar rests in the palm with the thumb wrapping or adjacent (a full grip is recommended for safety).
- **Shoulders (glenohumeral):** at the bottom the humeri hang in neutral abduction (~10–15°) and the shoulder girdle is depressed. During the pull the shoulders extend and adduct as the elbows flex and draw down toward the ribs.
- **Elbows:** flex progressively as the reach shortens. At the top the elbows are bent ~90° or less and drawn down beside the thorax. The forearms stay roughly vertical under the bar.
- **Wrists/hands:** fixed on the bar; the hands are the anchor. The wrists are in neutral extension (not flexed or hyperextended).
- **Muscles (primary):** latissimus dorsi (shoulder extension/adduction), biceps brachii (elbow flexion), brachialis, rhomboids and middle trapezius (scapular retraction), lower trapezius and serratus anterior (scapular depression/upward rotation control).

## 7. Lower Body
- The hips and knees are extended or slightly flexed; the legs hang with a controlled, slight forward pendulum.
- The pelvis is neutral (not anteriorly tilted); the gluteal muscles are gently engaged to prevent lumbar hyperextension.
- The feet are relaxed and pointed or plantarflexed naturally; they do not drive the movement.
- The lower body's role is to act as a stable, non-swinging mass that the upper body pulls against.

## 8. Contacts
- **Primary (anterior/upper) contact:** both hands fixed on the horizontal bar. The hands are the only external anchor; the body is lifted relative to them.
- **Secondary contact:** none required. The feet are free and hanging.
- **Contact stability:** the grip must be secure and static throughout—the hands do not slide, rotate, or regrip during the repetition.
- **Bottom reference:** the bottom (dead/active hang) has the arms fully extended and the shoulders depressed (not shrugged), with the feet hanging free.

## 9. Range of Motion
- **Shoulder girdle:** full scapular depression and retraction available at the top; full elevation and protraction at the bottom hang.
- **Glenohumeral:** from full shoulder flexion/abduction in the hang (~160–180° of elevation) to ~10–20° of residual elevation at the top (arms drawn down).
- **Elbow:** from ~175–180° (fully extended) at the bottom to ~70–100° of flexion at the top.
- **Wrist:** neutral (~0–20° extension) throughout.
- **Spine/trunk:** maintained within a few degrees of neutral sagittal alignment; no rotation.
- **Vertical travel:** the chin must clear the bar at the top; the bottom is the fully extended hang.

## 10. Muscle Intent
- **Initiation:** lower trapezius and serratus anterior depress the scapulae; rhomboids and middle trapezius retract them. The body rises a few units with arms straight.
- **Pull:** latissimus dorsi drives shoulder extension/adduction; biceps brachii and brachialis flex the elbows; the chest (pectoralis minor assisting scapular motion) leads upward.
- **Stabilization:** the anterior core (rectus abdominis, obliques) and glutes brace the trunk; the forearm flexors and grip muscles maintain the fixed hand contact; the scapular upward rotators control the path.

## 11. Visual Checkpoints
- [ ] ✓ Hands fixed on the bar, overhand grip, roughly shoulder-width (~1.35× shoulder).
- [ ] ✓ Arms fully extended at the bottom; shoulders depressed, not shrugged toward the ears.
- [ ] ✓ The first motion of the pull is the scapulae dropping and squeezing together (body rises a few units, elbows still straight).
- [ ] ✓ As the pull continues, the elbows flex and draw down toward the ribs.
- [ ] ✓ Forearms stay roughly vertical beneath the bar.
- [ ] ✓ The chest leads upward toward the bar, not the chin alone poking forward.
- [ ] ✓ The chin clears the bar at the top by a small margin.
- [ ] ✓ The trunk stays rigid; no swinging, no lumbar arch, no anterior pelvic tilt.
- [ ] ✓ The head is neutral; the neck is long, not jutted or tucked.
- [ ] ✓ The legs hang with a slight, controlled forward pendulum; feet are relaxed.
- [ ] ✓ The gaze is forward/down at the bottom, lifting naturally as the chest rises.
- [ ] ✓ The bottom position is a full dead/active hang with depressed shoulders.
- [ ] ✓ No visible shrugging of the upper traps at the top.
- [ ] ✓ The movement is smooth: scapular set → elbow flex → chest to bar.
- [ ] ✓ The same path is retraced on the descent (controlled, not dropped).

## 12. Common Mistakes
- **Shrugging (elevated scapulae):** letting the upper traps pull the shoulders toward the ears instead of depressing—reduces lat and scapular engagement and stresses the neck.
- **Kipping/swinging:** using momentum from the legs and lumbar spine to cheat the pull; removes the vertical-pull strength demand.
- **Chin poke only:** leading with the chin/jaw rather than the chest, which means the shoulders never fully extend and the lats are underused.
- **Elbows flaring forward/out too early:** beginning elbow flexion before the scapulae have set, losing the scapular-initiation sequence.
- **Partial range:** not reaching a full dead hang at the bottom or not clearing the chin at the top.
- **Lumbar hyperextension:** arching the low back and anteriorly tilting the pelvis instead of bracing the trunk.
- **Grip drift:** hands sliding or regripping mid-rep, or a thumbless "false" grip when a full grip is safer.

## 13. Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] Hands are fixed on the bar in an overhand (pronated) grip at roughly shoulder-width (~1.35× biacromial width).
- [ ] At the bottom the arms are fully extended and the shoulders are depressed (not shrugged).
- [ ] The pull begins with scapular depression and retraction (body rises with arms essentially straight) before elbow flexion.
- [ ] The elbows flex and draw down; the chest leads up to the bar.
- [ ] At the top the chin clears the bar.
- [ ] The trunk remains rigid and neutral (no swing, no lumbar arch, neutral pelvis).
- [ ] The feet hang free; the legs show at most a slight controlled forward pendulum.
- [ ] The descent retraces the same controlled path to a full hang.

# Plank (Standard) — Biomechanical Pose Specification

## 1. Exercise Overview

- **Movement goal:** A static, prone, closed-chain trunk hold in which the body is maintained as a single rigid line from the shoulders to the ankles, supported by the forearms or straight arms and the balls of the feet, with no movement beyond isometric bracing.
- **Training goal:** Develop trunk and scapular stability, anti-extension and anti-rotation control of the core, and isometric endurance of the abdominal wall, gluteals, and shoulder girdle.
- **Intended muscles:** Rectus abdominis, transverse abdominis, internal/external obliques (isometric), erector spinae (isometric neutral), gluteus maximus, quadriceps, serratus anterior, anterior deltoid (if straight-arm), trapezius (scapular setting).
- **Common execution style:** From a prone position, the elbows (or hands) are placed under the shoulders and the toes tucked; the body is lifted into a straight line and held, breathing steadily, with the trunk braced and the pelvis level.

## 2. Pose Purpose

- **Why it exists:** The standard plank is the foundational isometric trunk-stability hold; it trains the ability to maintain a neutral spine against gravity's attempt to sag the lumbar and drop the pelvis, and sets the baseline alignment for all push-up-family movement.
- **Canonical sub-poses:**
  - **Straight-arm (high) plank** — support on the hands, arms extended.
  - **Forearm plank** — support on the forearms, elbows under shoulders (see also Plank (Forearm)).
  - The hold is static; the "transition" is simply the entry/exit into the braced line.
- **Scapular strategy:** Scapulae protracted (serratus anterior) and depressed, flat against the rib cage; the shoulder girdle is set and stable, not collapsed or shrugged.

## 3. Whole-Body Alignment

- **Center of mass:** Located mid-torso, projected vertically between the forearm/hand base and the toe contacts; held steady.
- **Balance:** Stable, bilateral, symmetrical.
- **Symmetry:** Mirror-symmetric; equal load both arms and both feet.
- **Posture:** Neutral spine; a single straight line from acromion through greater trochanter to lateral malleolus.
- **Visual appearance:** A rigid board held off the floor; the body reads as one straight line from shoulders to ankles; no sag at the hips, no butt-up.

## 4. Head

- **Neck:** Neutral, continuation of the spine.
- **Gaze:** At the floor a short distance ahead.
- **Chin:** Slightly tucked.
- **Cervical alignment:** Smooth single curve.

## 5. Spine

- **Lumbar:** Neutral (normal lordosis), braced, not rounded (sag) or arched (hyperextended).
- **Thoracic:** Neutral; flat upper back, scapulae flush.
- **Rib cage:** Closed/down; no flaring.
- **Pelvis:** Neutral and level; no anterior or posterior tilt, no rotation.
- **Summary state:** Neutral spine held isometrically for the duration of the hold.

## 6. Upper Body

- **Shoulders (glenohumeral):** In the straight-arm version the humerus is vertical (arms extended); in the forearm version the upper arm is vertical from elbow to shoulder. The shoulders are the front support pivot.
- **Scapula:** Protracted (serratus), depressed, flat; no winging, no shrug.
- **Clavicles:** Level, symmetric.
- **Elbows:** In the forearm version, flexed ~90° and directly under the shoulders; in the straight-arm version, extended.
- **Forearms:** In the forearm version, parallel and flat on the floor, palms down or fists; in the straight-arm version, vertical.
- **Wrists:** In the straight-arm version, extended and straight; in the forearm version, neutral (weight through the forearm).
- **Hands:** Flat (palms down) or fists in the forearm version; flat in the straight-arm version, fingers forward.
- **Expected visual appearance:** Shoulders broad and stable; forearms (or arms) vertical under the shoulders; upper back flat.

## 7. Lower Body

- **Hip:** Extended/neutral; extensors (gluteals, hamstrings) hold the pelvis level.
- **Femur:** In line with the trunk; thighs parallel to floor.
- **Knee:** Extended, straight, held by quadriceps.
- **Ankle:** Plantarflexed; weight on balls of feet (toe contact), heels lifted; or the tops of the feet may rest flat depending on preference, but the ankle is not the primary support.
- **Foot:** Toe/ball contact (or dorsal surface if feet flat); arches lifted.
- **Toe direction:** Pointed straight back; no rotation.
- **Weight distribution:** Roughly 60–70% on the arms/forearms, 30–40% on the toes; symmetric.

## 8. Contacts

- **Forearm/hand contact (×2):** Forearms flat (or palms/fists) under the shoulders; even pressure; stable front support. Visually: forearms parallel, elbows under shoulders (or hands flat, wrists under shoulders).
- **Toe contact (×2):** Balls of feet; light relative to arms; stable rear support. Visually: heels lifted, toes back.

## 9. Range of Motion

- **Expected ROM:** Minimal — an isometric hold. The only acceptable micro-motion is steady breathing; the spine and limb angles are held constant.
- **Acceptable variation:** Slight, controlled breathing movement; maintained neutral alignment throughout the target hold time.
- **Unacceptable variation:** Any sag of the lumbar/pelvis, butt-up, shoulder elevation, or loss of the straight line during the hold.

## 10. Muscle Intent

- **Visibly engaged:** Abdominal wall (braced, no sag), gluteus maximus (pelvis level), quadriceps (knees straight), serratus anterior (flat scapulae), anterior deltoid/trapezius (shoulder setting), erector spinae (neutral hold).

## 11. Visual Checkpoints

- [ ] Body one straight line shoulder–hip–ankle.
- [ ] Head neutral, gaze at floor ahead, chin tucked.
- [ ] Neutral spine; pelvis level (no sag, no butt-up).
- [ ] Rib cage closed.
- [ ] Scapulae flat, depressed, protracted, no winging/shrug.
- [ ] Elbows under shoulders (forearm version) or wrists under shoulders (straight-arm).
- [ ] Forearms parallel/flat (forearm version) or arms vertical (straight-arm).
- [ ] Knees straight, weight on balls of feet, heels up.
- [ ] Feet symmetric, toes back, no rotation.
- [ ] Symmetric load left/right.
- [ ] No torso rotation.
- [ ] Trunk rigid and motionless (save breathing).
- [ ] Shoulders set, not hunched.
- [ ] Neck long.
- [ ] Glutes engaged (pelvis level).
- [ ] No excessive lumbar arch.
- [ ] Alignment maintained for the hold duration.
- [ ] Breathing steady, not breath-held.
- [ ] Hips not higher than shoulders.

## 12. Common Mistakes

- **Sagging hips (lumbar drop):** Abdominal brace fails; pelvis drops, lumbar rounds. Visually a dip at the waist. Wrong — loads the lumbar spine, removes the stability challenge.
- **Butt-up:** Pelvis rises above the line. Visually inverted-V at hips. Wrong — shortens the lever, removes trunk work.
- **Shoulder elevation / shrug:** Traps engage, shoulders to ears. Visually hunched top. Wrong — wastes tension, strains neck.
- **Winging scapulae:** Medial borders lift. Wrong — unstable shoulder girdle.
- **Breath-holding:** Tension causes breath holding. Wrong — increases pressure, reduces endurance.
- **Head dropped/craned:** Broken neck line. Wrong — disrupts cervical alignment.

## 13. Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Straight-line plank maintained for the full hold.
- [ ] Neutral spine/pelvis; no sag, no butt-up.
- [ ] Head neutral, chin tucked, gaze at floor.
- [ ] Scapulae flat/depressed/protracted, no winging/shrug.
- [ ] Elbows (or wrists) under shoulders; forearms parallel or arms vertical.
- [ ] Knees straight, weight on balls of feet.
- [ ] Symmetric, no rotation, balanced load.
- [ ] Steady breathing; trunk motionless save breathing.

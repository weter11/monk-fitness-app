# Biomechanical Pose Specification — Half-Kneeling Stretch (Hold)

> **Canonical reference.** This document defines what a half-kneeling mobility
> hold should look like from a strict biomechanics, anatomy, and physical-therapy
> standpoint. It is implementation-independent and describes human movement only.
> It must remain valid even if the rendering system behind it is rewritten from
> scratch.
>
> Sources: sports biomechanics, physical-therapy literature, strength &
> conditioning practice, and human anatomy. Where expert opinion differs, the
> consensus is stated and the alternatives are noted.

---

## 1. Exercise Overview

**Movement goal.** The half-kneeling stretch (or half-kneel mobility hold) is a
static positional hold used as a general lower-body and trunk mobility/stance
exercise. The athlete is in a half-kneel — one knee on the floor, the other foot
planted forward with the front knee at ~90° — and emphasizes correct pelvic
positioning and opening through the hip and thorax. It shares the base position
of the hip-flexor stretch but is framed as a broader mobility/stance hold rather
than a single-target stretch.

**Training goal.** Develop and reinforce a stable half-kneel stance: pelvic
positioning (neutral/slightly-tucked), hip-opening on the kneeling side, thoracic
openness, and trunk anti-rotation/anti-flexion control. It is a mobility and
positioning hold, not a strength or isolated-stretch movement.

**Intended muscles (lengthened / opened).**

- Kneeling (rear) side: iliopsoas (psoas major + iliacus), rectus femoris (mild),
  and anterior hip structures as the rear hip extends.
- Front side: anterior hip in a neutral-to-open position.

**Intended muscles (engaged / stabilizing).**

- Gluteus maximus and abdominal wall (pelvic positioning).
- Quadriceps of the front thigh (knee extension hold).
- Trunk: erector spinae, obliques, gluteus medius (upright, level-pelvis,
  square-shoulder control).

**Common execution style.** The athlete drops into a half-kneel: rear knee on the
floor with the lower leg trailing back, front foot planted forward with the front
knee at ~90° tracking over the front toe. The pelvis is held neutral or gently
tucked, the trunk is tall and square, and the athlete holds the position,
optionally reaching arms overhead or performing gentle thoracic openers. Hold
30–120 s per side, breathing steadily.

## 2. Pose Purpose

This specification treats the **held half-kneel position** as the canonical
audited pose.

**Held position.** The athlete is in a half-kneel: the rear knee rests on the
floor (rear lower leg trailing back), and the front foot is planted forward with
the front knee flexed to ~90° and tracking over the front toe. The pelvis is
neutral or with a gentle posterior tilt; the rear hip is in extension (opened).
The trunk is upright and square to the pelvis. Weight is shared between the front
foot and the rear knee contact. The arms may rest at the sides, at the chest, or
reach overhead.

**Emphasis (distinct from the hip-flexor stretch).** While the hip flexors are
opened on the kneeling side, the framing here is the *general* half-kneel stance:
pelvic neutrality/slight tuck, thoracic openness, and square-shoulder trunk
control are the primary audit targets, not a maximal isolated hip-flexor
lengthening.

**Pelvic positioning.** Neutral-to-gently-tucked. The lumbar spine is neutral (not
arched). The pelvis is level and square.

**Trunk.** Upright, tall, square. Arms at sides, chest, or overhead.

**Hold.** Static, no bouncing.

---

## 3. Whole-Body Alignment

**Center of mass.** The whole-body COM sits over the base formed by the front
foot and the rear knee contact. Roughly centered, leaning slightly forward over
the front foot. Within this support region.

**Balance.** Load shared: front foot the larger share, rear knee the rear share.
Balanced in the sagittal plane.

**Symmetry.** Not mirror-symmetric (half-kneel). The pelvis is level side-to-side
(no contralateral hip drop) and square (facing forward, no rotation). The
shoulders are level and square to the pelvis.

**Posture.** Upright trunk. Pelvis neutral/slightly tucked; lumbar neutral. Front
thigh roughly horizontal; rear thigh vertical (below the rear hip, lower leg
trailing).

**Visual appearance.** From the side, an upright trunk over a front shin-vertical
leg and a rear knee on the floor with the lower leg trailing. From the front, the
pelvis is level, the front knee tracks over the front toe, and the shoulders are
square.

---

## 4. Head

**Neck.** The cervical spine is a neutral continuation of the thoracic spine. No
excessive flexion or extension.

**Gaze.** The eyes look forward, toward the horizon or slightly down. Level,
relaxed.

**Chin.** Neutral and slightly packed; ears roughly above the shoulders from the
side. The head does not jut forward.

**Cervical alignment.** The cervical curve preserved but not exaggerated; one
gentle, unbroken line. No gooseneck, no turtle tuck.

---

## 5. Spine

**Lumbar.** Neutral (or gently flattened by a mild posterior tilt). The natural
lumbar arch is maintained within neutral range — not excessively extended (arched)
and not rounded into flexion. Long.

**Thoracic.** Neutral-to-slightly-extended and tall/broad. No rounding. The rib
cage is stacked over the pelvis with the anterior core engaged. Open through the
chest.

**Rib cage.** Ribs down, anterior abdominal wall braced. Chest open, not flared.
The thoracic cage moves as a unit with the trunk.

**Pelvis.** Neutral or with a gentle posterior tilt. Level side-to-side and
square (no rotation). The gluteals and abdominals co-contract to hold the
position.

**State summary.** Lumbar: neutral (slight flattening acceptable, not arched).
Thoracic: neutral/tall/open. Pelvis: neutral-to-gently-tucked, level, square. The
pelvis opens the rear hip without arching the lumbar.

---

## 6. Upper Body

**Shoulders (glenohumeral).** Level, square to the pelvis, relaxed away from the
ears. Arms rest at the sides, at the chest, or reach overhead (common to open the
front of the body and reinforce thoracic extension). The humerus is unloaded and
does not shrug.

**Scapula.** Flat on the rib cage; no winging or excessive elevation. If arms
reach overhead, the scapulae elevate/upwardly rotate naturally but should not
wing.

**Clavicles.** Level and symmetric; do not ride up toward the neck.

**Elbows.** Free per the chosen arm style. No forced position required.

**Forearms / wrists / hands.** Relaxed, not clenched. No deviation implying
shoulder compensation.

**Note.** The upper body is postural; it must not collapse forward, twist, or
tilt the trunk. Overhead arms increase thoracic openness but do not change the
lower-body landmarks.

---

## 7. Lower Body

**Rear hip.** In extension (rear thigh below/behind the rear hip, knee on the
floor, lower leg trailing). The rear hip is opened.

**Rear knee.** Flexed (~90°+) and resting on the floor, under or slightly behind
the rear hip. The rear support point.

**Rear ankle / foot.** The rear lower leg trails back along the floor; the rear
foot is relaxed (dorsum near the floor) and bears no driving load.

**Front hip.** Flexed to ~90° so the front thigh is roughly horizontal. Neutral-to-
open position.

**Front knee.** Flexed to ~90° and tracking over the front foot/toe. No valgus,
no varus. Front shin near-vertical.

**Front ankle.** Dorsiflexed; the front foot flat with the heel planted.

**Front foot.** Flat on the floor — heel and toe both down — pointing forward (or
very slightly outward). Primary load-bearing contact (with the rear knee).

**Weight distribution.** Shared between the front foot (larger share) and the
rear knee contact.

---

## 8. Contacts

**Front foot–floor contact (×1, primary).**

- *Contact surface:* the whole sole — heel and toe/ball — flat on the floor.
- *Expected pressure:* the larger load share; heaviest under the heel/midfoot
  through the toe; knee tracks over this foot.
- *Stability:* fixed. The front foot does not slide or lift.
- *Visual appearance:* flat foot, heel down, toes forward, knee tracking over
  the toes.

**Rear knee–floor contact (×1, primary rear).**

- *Contact surface:* the rear knee (patellar region / prepatellar soft tissue) on
  the floor.
- *Expected pressure:* a meaningful rear share; stabilized by the trailing lower
  leg.
- *Stability:* fixed. The rear knee does not slide.
- *Visual appearance:* rear knee on the floor under/behind the rear hip; lower
  leg trailing back.

No hand contact with the floor in the canonical half-kneel hold (hands at sides,
chest, or overhead). Front-knee or torso contact with the floor is absent. No
rear-shin prop contact (distinguishes it from the couch stretch).

---

## 9. Range of Motion

**Expected ROM.**

- Rear hip: extended (thigh behind/below the pelvis) with the knee flexed; the
  kneeling-side hip is opened.
- Rear knee: flexed ~90°+, knee on floor; rear lower leg trails.
- Front hip: flexed ~90° (thigh horizontal).
- Front knee: flexed ~90°, tracking over the toe.
- Front ankle: dorsiflexed, heel down.
- Spine: lumbar neutral, thoracic tall/open; little other change.

**Acceptable variation.**

- Front foot further or closer (closer increases rear-hip opening).
- Rear knee slightly behind the rear hip.
- Arms at sides, at chest, or overhead.
- Gentle posterior tilt vs strict neutral (both acceptable; no anterior tilt).
- Slight individual variation in neutral lumbar curvature.

**Unacceptable variation.**

- Anterior pelvic tilt / lumbar arch.
- Front knee valgus/varus or front heel lifting.
- Rear knee lifting off the floor.
- Pelvis drop, rotation, or side tilt.
- Torso collapsing forward or twisting.
- Rear foot taking a driving load that lifts the rear knee.
- Asymmetric shoulder tilt / loss of thoracic openness.

---

## 10. Muscle Intent

Structures that should *visually* appear lengthened / opened (rear side):

- **Iliopsoas (psoas major + iliacus)** — opened at the front of the rear hip as
  the rear hip extends.
- **Rectus femoris** — mild opening (rear knee flexed, hip extended).
- **Anterior hip / TFL / sartorius** — secondary opening.

Muscles that should *visually* appear engaged (to hold the stance):

- **Gluteus maximus (rear side)** — co-contracts to maintain pelvic position and
  rear-hip extension.
- **Rectus abdominis / obliques** — pelvic control / abdominal bracing.
- **Quadriceps (front thigh)** — knee extension hold of the front leg.
- **Erector spinae** — upright thoracic posture / openness.
- **Gluteus medius (both sides)** — pelvic leveling and square-shoulder control.

The body reads as "stable half-kneel: neutral/tucked pelvis, open thorax, upright
trunk, front leg stacked."

## 11. Visual Checkpoints

An auditor should be able to confirm each of the following from the rendered
pose:

1. ✓ Rear knee is on the floor, under or slightly behind the rear hip.
2. ✓ Rear lower leg trails back along the floor; rear foot not driving load.
3. ✓ Rear hip is in extension (thigh below/behind the pelvis); hip opened.
4. ✓ Front foot is flat, heel down, pointing forward.
5. ✓ Front knee flexed ~90°, tracking over the front toe.
6. ✓ Front knee shows no valgus or varus.
7. ✓ Front shin near-vertical; front heel on the floor.
8. ✓ Front thigh roughly horizontal.
9. ✓ Pelvis is neutral or gently tucked (not anteriorly tilted).
10. ✓ Lumbar spine is neutral (not arched, not rounded into flexion).
11. ✓ Thoracic spine tall and open — no rounding.
12. ✓ Rib cage stacked and braced; chest open.
13. ✓ Pelvis level side-to-side — no contralateral hip drop.
14. ✓ Pelvis square to the front — no rotation.
15. ✓ Neck neutral continuation of the spine; chin neutral and packed.
16. ✓ Gaze forward.
17. ✓ Shoulders level and square to the pelvis, not shrugged.
18. ✓ Scapulae flat — no winging.
19. ✓ No trunk twist; shoulders and hips face the same direction.
20. ✓ Front foot does not slide or lift.
21. ✓ Rear knee does not slide; rear knee stays on the floor.
22. ✓ The stance reads as a stable, opened half-kneel.
23. ✓ Torso upright (or very slight lean), not collapsed forward.
24. ✓ Weight shared between front foot and rear knee contact.
25. ✓ Arms positioned consistently (sides / chest / overhead) without distorting
     the trunk.
26. ✓ Overall silhouette: upright, open trunk, front shin vertical, rear knee
     down with lower leg trailing.
27. ✓ The held position is static (no bouncing).
28. ✓ No anterior pelvic tilt / lumbar arch.
29. ✓ Rear foot not bearing a driving load that lifts the rear knee.
30. ✓ The rendered pose matches the visual checkpoints in §11.

## 12. Common Mistakes

**Anterior pelvic tilt / arched lumbar ("butt out").** The athlete arches the
lower back and tilts the pelvis anteriorly. *Why wrong:* off-loads the opened hip
and stresses the lumbar. *Visual difference:* the lower back bows, the butt pokes
back.

**Front heel lifting.** The front heel rises. *Why wrong:* reduces front-leg
stability. *Visual difference:* front foot rolls onto the toe; front shin pitches
forward.

**Front knee valgus / not tracking the toe.** The front knee caves inward. *Why
wrong:* medial knee stress. *Visual difference:* front knee inside the toe line;
ankle rolls in.

**Pelvis drop / rotate.** The trailing-side hip drops or the pelvis rotates. *Why
wrong:* poor pelvic control. *Visual difference:* hips not level/square; one
iliac crest lower or rotated forward.

**Rounded upper back / forward collapse.** The torso pitches forward. *Why wrong:*
reduces thoracic openness and loads the lumbar. *Visual difference:* shoulders
ahead of the hips; upper back rounds.

**Rear knee lifting off the floor.** The rear knee comes up. *Why wrong:* reduces
rear-hip opening and the stance anchor. *Visual difference:* the rear thigh leaves
the vertical; the body rises.

**Twisting torso / uneven shoulders.** Shoulders rotate or tilt. *Why wrong:*
poor trunk control. *Visual difference:* shoulders not square to the pelvis.

**Rear foot driving load (lifting the rear knee).** The rear foot pushes so the
rear knee leaves the floor. *Why wrong:* defeats the kneeling anchor. *Visual
difference:* the rear knee is off the floor; weight shifts rearward.

## 13. Acceptance Criteria

The half-kneeling stretch (held position) is **accepted only if every criterion
is satisfied**:

- [ ] Rear knee on the floor, under or slightly behind the rear hip.
- [ ] Rear lower leg trailing; rear foot not bearing a driving load.
- [ ] Rear hip in extension; rear hip visibly opened.
- [ ] Front foot flat, heel down, forward, not sliding.
- [ ] Front knee flexed ~90°, tracking over the front toe, no valgus/varus.
- [ ] Front shin near-vertical; front heel on the floor.
- [ ] Front thigh roughly horizontal.
- [ ] Pelvis neutral or gently tucked (no anterior tilt); lumbar neutral.
- [ ] Thoracic spine tall and open; no rounding.
- [ ] Rib cage stacked and braced; chest open.
- [ ] Pelvis level and square; no drop or rotation.
- [ ] Neck continues the spinal line; chin neutral and packed.
- [ ] Shoulders level, square, not shrugged; scapulae flat.
- [ ] No trunk twist; shoulders and hips face the same direction.
- [ ] Gaze forward.
- [ ] Rear knee stays on the floor; front foot does not slide or lift.
- [ ] The stance reads as a stable, opened half-kneel.
- [ ] The rendered pose matches the visual checkpoints in §11.

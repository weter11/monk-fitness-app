# Pull-Up (Neutral Grip)

## 1. Exercise Overview
The neutral-grip pull-up is a closed-chain vertical pull performed on parallel neutral handles (palms facing each other) with the hands placed at roughly shoulder width. The neutral forearm position is the most joint-friendly wrist and shoulder orientation for a vertical pull and allows the elbows to track tightly to the ribs. It is often the most "lat- and biceps-friendly" pull-up variation because the biomechanics permit a strong elbow-flexion and shoulder-adduction path with minimal compensatory stress.

## 2. Pose Purpose
This document specifies the biomechanics of a single neutral-grip pull-up repetition, from the extended hang to the top. The palms face one another; the elbows tuck tight to the ribs and drive down and slightly forward; the sternum leads upward. The scapulae still initiate the motion (depress + retract), but because the grip is narrow and neutral, the elbows flex early and efficiently, making the pull feel compact and biceps/lat-dominant. The chin clears the bar at the top.

## 3. Whole-Body Alignment
- The body hangs in a vertical line beneath the neutral handles; the hands are at shoulder width, so the upper arms hang close to the torso.
- The trunk is braced and neutral; a slight, controlled forward leg pendulum is permitted but must not swing the torso.
- The midline stays in a single sagittal plane; no thoracic rotation.
- The compact elbow path keeps the upper arms close to the sides, giving a "narrow" silhouette compared to wide or standard grips.

## 4. Head
- Cervical spine neutral relative to the thorax; chin neither tucked nor jutted.
- Gaze forward and slightly downward at the bottom, lifting as the sternum rises; chin clears the bar at the top.
- Neck relaxed; no shoulder elevation toward the ears.

## 5. Spine
- Thoracic spine neutral to slight natural kyphosis; lumbar spine neutral.
- The pull initiates at the scapulae: from the relaxed, elevated, protracted hang, the lower trapezius and serratus anterior depress the scapulae while the rhomboids and middle trapezius retract them, raising the body a few units with arms essentially straight (active hang).
- Because the grip is neutral and narrow, the subsequent elbow-flexion phase begins smoothly and the chest (sternum) leads upward with the elbows hugging the ribs.
- The spine stays rigid; no flexion/extension beyond neutral, no rotation.

## 6. Upper Body
- **Grip:** neutral (palms facing each other) on parallel handles. Hands at shoulder width. Full grip.
- **Shoulders (glenohumeral):** humeri hang near-neutral abduction (~10–15°) and track close to the torso during the pull. Shoulders extend and adduct as the elbows flex and draw down/forward.
- **Elbows:** flex and tuck tightly to the ribs, driving down and slightly forward (the classic "elbows to pockets" path). At the top the elbows are bent ~70–100° and pinned to the sides.
- **Wrists/hands:** fixed on the handles (palms inward); wrists in neutral (the neutral grip spares the wrists versus pronated/supinated positions).
- **Muscles (primary):** latissimus dorsi (adduction/extension), biceps brachii and brachialis (elbow flexion, efficiently loaded by the neutral path), pectoralis major (sternal, assists bringing the humerus down/forward), rhomboids/middle trapezius (retraction), lower trapezius/serratus anterior (depression).

## 7. Lower Body
- Hips and knees extended or slightly flexed; legs hang with a slight controlled forward pendulum.
- Pelvis neutral; glutes gently engaged to prevent lumbar hyperextension.
- Feet relaxed, not driving the movement.
- The lower body is a stable, non-swinging mass.

## 8. Contacts
- **Primary (anterior/upper) contact:** both hands fixed on the neutral handles, at shoulder width. The hands are the only external anchor; the body is lifted relative to them.
- **Secondary contact:** none. Feet are free.
- **Contact stability:** the grip is secure and static; hands do not slide or regrip.
- **Bottom reference:** full extended hang, arms straight, shoulders depressed (not shrugged), feet hanging free.

## 9. Range of Motion
- **Shoulder girdle:** full depression and retraction at top; full elevation and protraction at bottom.
- **Glenohumeral:** from high shoulder elevation in the hang to near-neutral at the top, with the humerus staying close to the torso.
- **Elbow:** from ~175–180° (extended) at bottom to ~70–100° of flexion at top (elbows tucked to ribs).
- **Wrist:** neutral (the neutral grip keeps the wrist in its most natural position).
- **Spine/trunk:** neutral sagittal alignment; no rotation.
- **Vertical travel:** chin clears the bar/handles at the top; bottom is the full hang.

## 10. Muscle Intent
- **Initiation:** lower trapezius + serratus anterior depress; rhomboids + middle trapezius retract; body rises with arms straight.
- **Pull:** latissimus dorsi adducts/extends the humerus; biceps/brachialis flex the elbows with an efficient neutral-path load; pectoralis major draws the upper arm down and slightly forward so the sternum leads up.
- **Stabilization:** anterior core and glutes brace the trunk; forearm flexors and grip maintain the fixed contact; scapular upward rotators control the path.

## 11. Visual Checkpoints
- [ ] ✓ Hands fixed on neutral handles, palms facing each other, at shoulder width.
- [ ] ✓ Arms fully extended at the bottom; shoulders depressed, not shrugged.
- [ ] ✓ The first motion is the scapulae dropping and squeezing together (body rises a few units, elbows straight).
- [ ] ✓ The elbows tuck tight to the ribs and drive down and slightly forward.
- [ ] ✓ The sternum (chest) leads upward toward the bar.
- [ ] ✓ The chin clears the bar at the top.
- [ ] ✓ The trunk stays rigid; no swing, no lumbar arch, neutral pelvis.
- [ ] ✓ The head is neutral; neck long, not jutted.
- [ ] ✓ The legs hang with a slight controlled forward pendulum.
- [ ] ✓ Gaze forward/down at bottom, lifting naturally.
- [ ] ✓ Bottom position is a full hang with depressed shoulders.
- [ ] ✓ No visible upper-trap shrugging at the top.
- [ ] ✓ The path is smooth: scapular set → elbows tuck to ribs → chest to bar.
- [ ] ✓ Descent retraces the same controlled path.

## 12. Common Mistakes
- **Elbows flaring out:** letting the elbows drift away from the ribs defeats the compact neutral-grip path and shifts load off the lats/biceps.
- **Shrugging:** elevating the shoulders instead of depressing them.
- **Swinging/kipping:** using momentum rather than the strict vertical pull.
- **Chest not leading:** pulling only with the arms and leaving the sternum behind, reducing lat/pec involvement.
- **Partial range:** not reaching a full hang or not clearing the chin.
- **Lumbar hyperextension:** arching instead of bracing the trunk.

## 13. Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] Hands are fixed on neutral handles in a palms-facing-each-other grip at shoulder width.
- [ ] At the bottom the arms are fully extended and the shoulders depressed (not shrugged).
- [ ] The pull begins with scapular depression and retraction before elbow flexion.
- [ ] The elbows tuck tightly to the ribs and drive down and slightly forward.
- [ ] The sternum leads up; the chin clears the bar at the top.
- [ ] The trunk remains rigid and neutral; no swing, no lumbar arch.
- [ ] The feet hang free with at most a slight controlled forward pendulum.
- [ ] The descent retraces the same controlled path to a full hang.

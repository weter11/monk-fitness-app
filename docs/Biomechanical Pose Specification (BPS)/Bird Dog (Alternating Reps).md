# Bird Dog (Alternating Reps) — Biomechanical Pose Specification

## 1. Exercise Overview
The Bird Dog performed as alternating repetitions uses the same quadruped base as the static hold, but the contralateral reach is cycled: one arm and the opposite leg extend, return to neutral tabletop, then the other arm and opposite leg extend, and so on. The exercise is a dynamic expression of trunk stiffness — the spine and pelvis must stay neutral and still even as limbs move in and out of extension. It trains the same anti-rotation / anti-extension system as the hold, with added demands of control during limb transition.

Populations: beginners through advanced; used in core conditioning, warm-ups, and low-back rehabilitation progressions. No equipment required.

## 2. Pose Purpose
- Train the trunk to remain neutral, level, and unmoving while limbs alternately extend and retract.
- Develop contralateral coordination (opposite arm/leg) under load without rotating or tilting the pelvis.
- Build endurance of the deep stabilizers (multifidus, transversus abdominis, gluteus medius) across repeated cycles.
- Reinforce the "return to neutral" control between repetitions.

## 3. Whole-Body Alignment
- Base: quadruped (hands under shoulders, knees under hips) with a neutral, level trunk.
- At the top of each rep, one arm reaches forward and the contralateral leg reaches back, the trunk forming a horizontal beam (arm–trunk–leg in line).
- Between reps, the body returns fully to the four-point neutral tabletop (both hands and both knees grounded, spine neutral).
- The transition is smooth: the reaching limbs extend and retract along the floor line without dragging or swinging the trunk.
- At no point does the pelvis rotate or tilt, and the spine does not flex, extend beyond neutral, or rotate.

## 4. Head
- Head is a neutral continuation of the cervical spine throughout; gaze down and slightly forward.
- During the reach, the chin stays gently tucked (no cervical extension, no heavy forward hang).
- The neck does not rotate or side-bend; ears remain level over the shoulders.

## 5. Spine
- Spine stays neutral at all phases: natural lumbar lordosis and thoracic kyphosis preserved; back neither flattened nor arched.
- No spinal rotation (chest and pelvis stay square to the floor).
- No lateral flexion of the trunk toward either reaching limb.
- The return-to-neutral phase is as controlled as the reach — no momentum-driven sag or rounding.
- Pelvis level and neutral (ASIS horizontal) during both the reach and the return.

## 6. Upper Body
- Supporting arm: shoulder over wrist, elbow extended (not locked), scapula depressed and set, no winging.
- Reaching arm: straight, extended forward parallel to floor; shoulder in neutral ~180° flexion; scapula gently retracted, not shrugged.
- During the return, the arm folds back to the four-point base under control, shoulder returning to neutral over the wrist.
- Both shoulders stay level; the thoracic cage does not rotate.

## 7. Lower Body
- Supporting leg: hip and knee ~90°, shin vertical, thigh a stable pillar.
- Reaching leg: extended straight back, hip in neutral extension (thigh collinear with pelvis, parallel to floor), knee extended; reaches from the hip, not hiked.
- The reaching hip does not hike or externally rotate; the supporting knee stays centered over the hip.
- During the return, the leg folds back to the 90° tabletop under control.

## 8. Contacts
- Four-point base at the neutral return; three-point base (one hand + opposite knee) at the reach top.
- Hand contact: palm flat, fingers spread, weight through the carpal arch; wrist neutral.
- Knee contact: patella/shin on a padded surface, weight centered.
- The floor supports the grounded points; transitions never lift more than one hand and one knee simultaneously (and those are contralateral).

## 9. Range of Motion
- Reaching shoulder flexion: to ~180° (parallel to floor) at the top, returning to neutral over the wrist at the base.
- Reaching hip extension: to neutral extension (thigh in line with pelvis), returning to 90° flexion at the base.
- Reaching knee extension: full (~0°) at the top, returning to ~90° flexion (tabletop) at the base.
- Spine and pelvis: ROM is zero — neutral is maintained through every rep (the movement is in the limbs, not the trunk).
- Neck: mild flexion (~20–30°) sustained; no end-range motion.

## 10. Muscle Intent
- Transversus abdominis and internal obliques: continuous abdominal bracing (drawing-in + gentle hollow) across the cycle.
- Multifidus: segmental spinal co-contraction preserving neutral.
- Erector spinae: isometric hold of neutral extension against gravity.
- Gluteus maximus/medius (reaching + supporting hips): hip extension and pelvic leveling against drop.
- Shoulder stabilizers (reaching side): rotator cuff, serratus anterior, lower trapezius for a steady, level reach.
- Quadriceps (supporting leg): maintains the 90° stance and controls the return.

## 11. Visual Checkpoints
- [ ] Start each rep from a four-point neutral tabletop.
- [ ] Reach is contralateral: right arm with left leg, then left arm with right leg.
- [ ] Reaching arm parallel to floor, straight.
- [ ] Reaching leg parallel to floor, straight, reaching from the hip.
- [ ] Pelvis level throughout (no hip hike or drop).
- [ ] Spine neutral throughout (no arch, no round, no twist).
- [ ] Shoulders square to floor at the top of the reach.
- [ ] Hips square to floor at the top of the reach.
- [ ] Chin gently tucked; gaze down-forward.
- [ ] Supporting scapula flat (not winged).
- [ ] Reaching shoulder not shrugged.
- [ ] Return to neutral is controlled, no momentum flop.
- [ ] Only one hand and the opposite knee are airborne at the reach top.
- [ ] Wrists neutral, knees centered over hips.
- [ ] Breathing steady across reps (no holding).
- [ ] No trunk rotation as limbs alternate.
- [ ] Limbs extend along the floor line, not upward.
- [ ] Both ASIS horizontal when viewed from front at the reach.
- [ ] Neck aligned with neutral spine, no crane.
- [ ] Tempo is even on the way out and back.

## 12. Common Mistakes
- Using trunk rotation or pelvic twist to "help" the limb reach.
- Hiking the reaching hip to gain height instead of extending from the hip.
- Arching the low back at the top of the reach.
- Letting the spine sag or round during the return phase.
- Shrugging the reaching shoulder toward the ear.
- Dropping the head or craning it forward/up.
- Moving with momentum rather than controlled, braced transitions.
- Holding the breath instead of breathing rhythmically.
- Allowing the supporting knee to cave inward.

## 13. Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] Each rep alternates contralateral arm/leg reaches from a neutral four-point base.
- [ ] Spine and pelvis remain neutral and level (no rotation, tilt, or flexion/extension beyond neutral) across all reps.
- [ ] Reaching limbs extend parallel to the floor and return under control to neutral.
- [ ] Only one hand and the opposite knee are lifted at the reach top; the other three points stay grounded.
- [ ] Head and neck stay neutral and aligned with the spine.
- [ ] Movement is controlled and braced, with steady respiration, for the prescribed repetitions.

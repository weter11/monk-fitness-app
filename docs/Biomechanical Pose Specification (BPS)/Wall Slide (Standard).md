# Wall Slide (Standard)

## 1. Exercise Overview
The Wall Slide is a standing mobility and posture drill performed with the back against a wall. The arms are held in a "W" — upper arms at ~90° abduction, elbows bent ~90° — with the elbows and the backs of the hands/wrists touching the wall. The arms then slide up and down the wall while keeping contact, training overhead shoulder range, scapular control, and thoracic extension. The canonical sub-pose is the arms-raised-up position: elbows and wrists still on the wall, arms reaching overhead. It is widely used for shoulder-health, postural correction, and as a warm-up.

## 2. Pose Purpose
- Improve shoulder flexion/overhead range of motion with the scapulae controlled.
- Train scapular upward rotation and depression against the wall constraint.
- Encourage thoracic extension and an open chest (counter to rounded posture).
- Teach the low back to stay against the wall (lumbar control) during arm elevation.
- Provide a guided, low-load shoulder/scapular mobility drill.

## 3. Whole-Body Alignment
- Stand with the back (head, upper back, pelvis, and ideally low back) against a wall.
- Feet a few inches from the wall, knees soft.
- Arms in the "W" against the wall: upper arms ~45–90° from the trunk, elbows bent 90°, elbows and wrists/hands contacting the wall.
- As the arms slide up, the trunk stays stacked; the low back does not peel off the wall.
- The motion is slow and controlled; breathing is easy.

## 4. Head
- Head rests against the wall, in neutral alignment with the spine.
- The cervical spine is tall; the chin is level (not jutting, not tucked excessively).
- The head stays in contact with the wall through the slide.

## 5. Spine
- **Thoracic:** gently extended/open against the wall (the upper back contacts the wall — this is the posture goal).
- **Lumbar:** stays in contact with the wall (neutral); it must NOT arch away as the arms rise (a common compensation is the low back bowing off the wall).
- **Cervical:** neutral, against the wall.
- The whole spine is a stable, upright stack; the arms move around it.

## 6. Upper Body
- Arms start in the W with elbows and wrists on the wall.
- As the arms slide up overhead, the shoulders flex and the scapulae rotate upward and stay depressed (down, not shrugged).
- The elbows and wrists maintain wall contact throughout (this is the constraint that keeps the pattern honest).
- At the top (canonical sub-pose) the arms are overhead with elbows/wrists still on the wall.
- The chest stays open; the upper traps do not elevate the shoulders toward the ears.

## 7. Lower Body
- Feet a few inches from the wall, hip-width, knees soft.
- The pelvis is against the wall and level; the hips do not shift.
- Legs are quiet; they just ground the stance.
- The glutes may lightly engage to keep the pelvis and low back against the wall.

## 8. Contacts
- The back of the head, upper back, pelvis, and low back contact the wall.
- Elbows and the backs of the wrists/hands contact the wall (the guiding constraint).
- Feet on the floor.
- The wall is the prop that enforces posture and arm path.

## 9. Range of Motion
- Shoulder flexion: from the W (~90° abduction, 90° flexion) up to full overhead (arms straight up) as far as wall contact allows.
- Scapular upward rotation + depression: through the slide.
- Thoracic extension: maintained (upper back flat to wall).
- The end range is where the wrists can still stay on the wall; if they peel off, that is the current limit. Performed as up/down reps or held at the top.

## 10. Muscle Intent
- Lower and middle trapezius + serratus anterior: control scapular upward rotation and depression.
- Deltoids (especially anterior/middle) and shoulder flexors: elevate the arms.
- Rhomboids: keep the scapulae set as the arms rise.
- Erector spinae: keep the spine stacked against the wall.
- Glutes/core: keep the pelvis and low back against the wall.

## 11. Visual Checkpoints
- [ ] The back (head, upper back, pelvis) is against the wall.
- [ ] Arms start in a clear W with elbows and wrists on the wall.
- [ ] As the arms rise, elbows and wrists stay in contact with the wall.
- [ ] The low back stays against the wall (does not bow off).
- [ ] The shoulders stay down (not shrugged toward the ears) as the arms rise.
- [ ] The chest is open, upper back flat to the wall.
- [ ] The head stays against the wall, neutral, chin level.
- [ ] At the top the arms are overhead with wrists still on the wall (canonical sub-pose).
- [ ] Both arms move symmetrically.
- [ ] The pelvis stays level and against the wall.
- [ ] The movement is slow and controlled, not forced.
- [ ] Knees are soft, weight even on the feet.
- [ ] Breathing is smooth.
- [ ] No neck tension or upper-trap bulging.
- [ ] The range stops where wall contact is lost (honest limit).

## 12. Common Mistakes
- Letting the low back bow off the wall as the arms rise (lumbar arch compensation).
- Shrugging the shoulders up toward the ears.
- Letting the elbows or wrists peel off the wall (cheating the path).
- Rounding the upper back off the wall.
- Jutting the head forward off the wall.
- Forcing the arms higher than wall contact allows.
- Holding the breath.

## 13. Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] The spine (head, upper back, low back, pelvis) stays against the wall with the low back not bowing off.
- [ ] The arms slide from the W to overhead while elbows and wrists maintain wall contact.
- [ ] The shoulders remain depressed (not shrugged) and the chest stays open.
- [ ] The head is neutral against the wall and both arms move symmetrically.
- [ ] The movement is slow, controlled, and breathing-paced, stopping honestly at the wall-contact limit.

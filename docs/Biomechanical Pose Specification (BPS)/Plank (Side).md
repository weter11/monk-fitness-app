# Plank (Side) — Biomechanical Pose Specification

## 1. Exercise Overview

- **Movement goal:** A static, unilateral, closed-chain trunk hold performed in the frontal plane: the body is supported on one forearm and the side of one foot (or stacked feet), forming a straight line from the head to the feet, held isometrically with the trunk braced against lateral flexion.
- **Training goal:** Develop lateral core stability — anti-lateral-flexion control of the obliques and quadratus lumborum — plus shoulder and hip abduction/abduction-stability endurance.
- **Intended muscles:** Obliquus internus/externus (especially the working side), quadratus lumborum, gluteus medius/minimus (hip abduction hold), gluteus maximus, rectus abdominis (isometric), transverse abdominis, serratus anterior (scapular setting on the supporting arm), deltoid/trapezius (shoulder support).
- **Common execution style:** Lying on one side, the lower elbow is placed under the shoulder, the body is lifted so the hips come off the floor, and the body is held as a straight line from head to feet; the free arm is raised toward the ceiling or placed on the hip; the feet may be stacked (one on top of the other) or the top foot placed in front of the bottom foot for a wider base.

## 2. Pose Purpose

- **Why it exists:** The side plank isolates lateral trunk stability — the ability to resist gravity's pull into lateral flexion (hip drop) — training the obliques and quadratus lumborum that keep the pelvis and spine level in the frontal plane.
- **Canonical sub-poses:**
  - **Stacked-feet side plank** — top foot rests directly on top of the bottom foot; narrower, harder base.
  - **Staggered-feet side plank** — top foot placed in front of the bottom foot on the floor; wider, more stable base (regression).
  - The hold is static; the "transition" is entry/exit into the braced frontal-plane line.
- **Scapular strategy:** On the supporting side, the scapula is protracted and depressed, flat against the rib cage; the shoulder is set and stable.

## 3. Whole-Body Alignment

- **Center of mass:** Located mid-torso, projected vertically over the supporting forearm and the side-of-foot contact; held steady in the frontal plane.
- **Balance:** Stable on the unilateral base; the body is a straight diagonal from head to feet in the side view.
- **Symmetry:** The pose is intentionally unilateral — the working (lower) side bears the load; the body is symmetric about its own long axis (no twist), but left/right limbs differ by role.
- **Posture:** Neutral spine; the body forms one straight line from the crown of the head through the shoulder, hip, and ankle of the supporting side; the hips are lifted and level (no drop toward the floor).
- **Visual appearance:** A straight, rigid diagonal board in the frontal plane; from the front the body is a single line from head to feet with the hips pushed up; the free arm is raised or on the hip; no sag of the lower hip.

## 4. Head

- **Neck:** Neutral, continuation of the cervical–thoracic line; not craned up or dropped.
- **Gaze:** Forward or slightly up, along the body's plane; eyes relaxed.
- **Chin:** Neutral, not jutting.
- **Cervical alignment:** Smooth, in line with the straight body; no lateral tilt of the head beyond the body line.

## 5. Spine

- **Lumbar:** Neutral, braced against lateral flexion; not side-bent (no dip toward the floor on the unsupported side).
- **Thoracic:** Neutral; the upper body is a straight segment; the scapula of the supporting side is flat.
- **Rib cage:** Closed/down; the supporting-side rib cage is set.
- **Pelvis:** Level and lifted — the pelvis is raised off the floor and held parallel to the floor line (no anterior/posterior tilt, no drop of the lower hip).
- **Summary state:** Neutral spine held isometrically in the frontal plane; the trunk resists lateral flexion for the hold.

## 6. Upper Body

- **Shoulders (glenohumeral):** The supporting shoulder is abducted ~90° (arm perpendicular to floor in the forearm version); the free shoulder is relaxed or used to raise the arm.
- **Scapula (supporting side):** Protracted and depressed, flat against the rib cage; no winging, no shrug.
- **Clavicles:** The supporting-side clavicle is level; no elevation.
- **Elbows:** Flexed ~90° and directly under the supporting shoulder (forearm version); the forearm is perpendicular to the body line.
- **Forearms:** The supporting forearm lies flat on the floor, perpendicular to the torso, palm down or fist.
- **Wrists:** Neutral (weight through forearm); if straight-arm side plank, the wrist is extended under the shoulder.
- **Hands:** Flat or fist on the floor (supporting); the free hand is raised toward ceiling or on the hip.
- **Expected visual appearance:** One shoulder stacked over its elbow; the free arm vertical or on the hip; the supporting scapula flat; the shoulders aligned vertically over the elbow.

## 7. Lower Body

- **Hip:** Abducted/neutral on the supporting side; the hip abductors (gluteus medius/minimus) hold the pelvis level against gravity's lateral pull; the pelvis is lifted.
- **Femur:** The supporting leg is straight and in line with the trunk; in the stacked version the legs are together; in the staggered version the top leg is slightly forward.
- **Knee:** Extended, straight on the supporting leg.
- **Ankle:** The supporting foot is on its lateral (outer) edge / side, or flat with weight on the outer border; the foot is the rear base.
- **Foot:** Side-of-foot (lateral border) contact for the supporting foot; the top foot rests on or in front of it.
- **Toe direction:** Pointing along the body line (forward, away from the head) for the stacked version; the free foot may point forward or down.
- **Weight distribution:** Load on the supporting forearm and the side of the supporting foot; the free limbs are unloaded; symmetric effort left/right across reps (each side held).

## 8. Contacts

- **Supporting forearm contact (×1):** Forearm flat on floor, elbow under shoulder; even pressure; the front support. Visually: forearm perpendicular to body, elbow under shoulder.
- **Supporting foot contact (×1, lateral):** The outer (lateral) border of the foot on the floor; stable rear support. Visually: foot on its side, leg straight up to the hip.
- **(Free foot):** In the stacked version rests on top of the supporting foot; in the staggered version rests in front on the floor; not a primary load contact.

## 9. Range of Motion

- **Expected ROM:** Minimal — an isometric hold in the frontal plane. The body line is held constant; only steady breathing micro-motion is acceptable.
- **Acceptable variation:** Slight breathing motion; maintained neutral frontal-plane alignment for the hold.
- **Unacceptable variation:** Any drop of the lower hip toward the floor (lateral flexion), rotation/twist of the trunk out of the frontal plane, or shoulder collapse.

## 10. Muscle Intent

- **Visibly engaged:** Obliques (working side domes/braces), quadratus lumborum (pelvis lift), gluteus medius/minimus (hip abduction hold), gluteus maximus (pelvis level), rectus/transverse abdominis (braced), serratus anterior (flat scapula), deltoid/trapezius (shoulder support).

## 11. Visual Checkpoints

- [ ] Body one straight line head→shoulder→hip→ankle in the frontal plane.
- [ ] Lower hip lifted, NOT dropped toward the floor.
- [ ] Pelvis level, parallel to floor (no tilt, no rotation).
- [ ] Supporting elbow directly under the shoulder.
- [ ] Supporting forearm flat, perpendicular to body.
- [ ] Supporting scapula flat, depressed, no winging/shrug.
- [ ] Head neutral, in line with the body (no lateral tilt).
- [ ] Knees straight (supporting leg).
- [ ] Supporting foot on its lateral border, stable.
- [ ] Feet stacked or staggered symmetrically per chosen variant.
- [ ] Free arm raised or on hip (not dangling).
- [ ] No twist/rotation of the trunk out of the frontal plane.
- [ ] Trunk rigid and motionless (save breathing).
- [ ] Symmetric effort when both sides held.
- [ ] Neutral spine; no lateral sag.
- [ ] Shoulders set, not hunched.
- [ ] Neck long.
- [ ] Glutes engaged (pelvis level).
- [ ] Breathing steady.
- [ ] Alignment maintained for the hold duration.

## 12. Common Mistakes

- **Hip drop (lower hip sagging):** The abdominal/oblique brace fails and the lower hip falls toward the floor; visually the body line bends at the waist. Wrong — this is exactly the lateral-flexion fault the exercise trains against; loads the spine.
- **Twisting out of the frontal plane:** The trunk rotates so the chest faces forward instead of sideways; visually the body turns. Wrong — shifts the challenge away from lateral stability.
- **Shoulder collapse / shrug:** The supporting shoulder drops toward the ear or the elbow drifts forward; visually a hunched or collapsed top. Wrong — unstable shoulder, neck strain.
- **Stacked feet too unstable (advanced):** Beginners attempt stacked feet and wobble; visually the base rolls. Wrong for novices — use staggered feet.
- **Head craned up:** Neck breaks the line; visually a kink. Wrong — cervical strain.
- **Breath-holding:** Visually tense, still chest. Wrong — reduces endurance.

## 13. Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Straight head-to-ankle line in the frontal plane maintained.
- [ ] Lower hip lifted and level; no sag toward floor.
- [ ] Supporting elbow under shoulder; scapula flat/depressed, no winging/shrug.
- [ ] Knees straight; supporting foot on lateral border, stable.
- [ ] No trunk rotation out of the frontal plane.
- [ ] Free arm raised or on hip; head neutral.
- [ ] Neutral spine; glutes engaged; symmetric effort per side.
- [ ] Steady breathing; alignment held for the duration.

# Kettlebell Swing (Backpack)

## 1. Exercise Overview
The Kettlebell Swing (here loaded with a backpack or similar implement) is a hip-hinge power exercise. The load is swung from between the legs up to roughly chest/eye height by explosive hip extension, then allowed to fall back down as the hips hinge. Crucially it is a HINGE (the knees bend only slightly; the movement is driven by the hips folding and extending), not a squat. The canonical sub-poses are (a) the HINGE BOTTOM — torso folded forward over the hinged hips with the load behind the knees, and (b) the TOP — body tall, hips fully extended, slight knee bend, trunk upright-ish, load at height. It develops posterior-chain power (glutes, hamstrings) and trunk bracing.

## 2. Pose Purpose
- Develop explosive hip extension power (glutes, hamstrings).
- Train the hip-hinge pattern distinct from squatting.
- Strengthen the posterior chain and trunk bracing under dynamic load.
- Improve conditioning and power output (a ballistic, metabolic exercise).
- Teach the pendulum rhythm of load acceleration/deceleration via the hips.

## 3. Whole-Body Alignment
- Standing, feet roughly hip-to-shoulder width, toes slightly out, knees soft.
- The spine stays neutral (braced) in BOTH the hinge bottom and the top — it never rounds.
- The hinge is at the hips: the torso folds forward over the pelvis while the hips push back.
- The arms are merely a strap — they hold the load but do not curl or press it; the power comes from the hips.
- The trunk is rigid; the movement is a hip fold-and-extend with the load swinging.

## 4. Head
- Head is in neutral alignment with the spine in both phases ("eyes forward / chest proud" at the top; "eyes forward" looking slightly down at the bottom).
- The cervical spine follows the thoracic line; the head does not drop heavily or crane up.
- At the top the head is tall; at the bottom the head is in line with the hinged torso.

## 5. Spine
- **Whole spine:** neutral and braced throughout — a rigid cylinder.
- **Hinge bottom:** the lumbar and thoracic stay long and flat as the torso inclines forward (~45° or more) over the hinged hips; the flexion is at the hip joint, NOT spinal flexion.
- **Top:** the spine returns to upright/neutral as the hips extend; no overarching.
- The core (rectus, obliques, erector spinae, transversus) braces to prevent any spinal movement.

## 6. Upper Body
- The arms hold the load (backpack straps or the implement handle) with straight elbows; they are relaxed conduits, not active lifters.
- At the top the shoulders are down (not shrugged) and the load is steered to chest/eye height by the hip thrust, not an arm raise.
- The lats engage to "pack" the shoulders and protect the spine.
- The chest is proud/open at the top; the upper back is flat at the bottom.

## 7. Lower Body
- **Hinge bottom:** hips push back and down, the torso folds forward, knees bend only slightly (the shin angle barely changes); the load swings behind the knees.
- **Top:** hips drive to full extension (glutes squeezed), knees nearly straight (slight soft bend), the body stands tall.
- The weight shifts onto the mid-foot/heel in the hinge; the toes stay planted.
- The movement is driven by the posterior chain; the quads contribute only the slight knee bend.

## 8. Contacts
- Feet flat on the floor (mid-foot/heel dominant in the hinge).
- Hands grip the load (backpack straps or handle) — a load contact, not a fixed anchor.
- The load itself swings freely; at the bottom it is behind the knees near the floor but not necessarily touching.
- No other body contact; the exercise is freestanding.

## 9. Range of Motion
- Hip hinge: from upright (top, hips extended) to a forward-fold hinge (bottom) — hip flexion of roughly 90°+ at the joint while the spine stays neutral.
- Knee flexion: only slight (a few degrees more than the top) — this distinguishes the swing from a squat.
- Shoulder: the arms travel from low (between legs) to ~horizontal/chest height (top) — driven by the hips, not shoulder flexion.
- The pendulum is cyclic; tempo is explosive on the upswing, controlled on the downswing.

## 10. Muscle Intent
- Gluteus maximus and hamstrings: the prime movers of the explosive hip extension (top).
- Erector spinae and deep core: brace the neutral spine against the load and the hinge.
- Latissimus dorsi: pack the shoulders and connect the arms to the trunk.
- Quadriceps: control the slight knee bend (minimal compared to a squat).
- Hip flexors and hamstrings (eccentric): decelerate the load on the downswing into the hinge.
- Calves: stabilize the foot through the drive.

## 11. Visual Checkpoints
- [ ] The stance is stable, feet hip-to-shoulder width, knees soft.
- [ ] The spine is neutral and braced in BOTH the hinge bottom and the top (never rounded).
- [ ] The hinge is at the hips — torso folds forward, knees bend only slightly (canonical bottom).
- [ ] The load swings behind the knees at the bottom, not in front.
- [ ] At the top the hips are fully extended, body tall, load at chest/eye height (canonical top).
- [ ] The arms are straight — they do not curl or press the load.
- [ ] The shoulders are packed/down, not shrugged.
- [ ] The head is neutral, in line with the spine in each phase.
- [ ] The chest is proud/open at the top; the back is flat at the bottom.
- [ ] The weight stays over the mid-foot/heel in the hinge.
- [ ] The movement is driven by the hips, not the arms or a squat.
- [ ] The knees track over the toes and do not cave inward.
- [ ] Breathing is rhythmic (inhale into the hinge, exhale on the thrust).
- [ ] The load path is a pendulum, not a squat-up.
- [ ] Both phases are controlled, not flailing at the top.

## 12. Common Mistakes
- Squatting instead of hinging (too much knee bend, thighs dropping).
- Rounding the low back at the bottom (loss of spinal brace).
- Using the arms to curl/press the load up instead of thrusting with the hips.
- Letting the load drift in front of the body rather than behind the knees.
- Over-arching the low back at the top.
- Shrugging the shoulders up.
- Letting the knees cave inward or the heels lift.

## 13. Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] The movement is a hip hinge (slight knee bend) with a neutral, braced spine in both the bottom and top positions.
- [ ] The canonical hinge bottom shows the torso folded forward over the hips with the load behind the knees.
- [ ] The canonical top shows full hip extension, a tall trunk, and the load at chest/eye height driven by the hips.
- [ ] The arms remain straight (conduits), shoulders packed, and the head neutral.
- [ ] The rhythm is hip-driven and breathing-paced, with no spinal rounding or squatting compensation.

# Burpee (Standard)

## 1. Exercise Overview
The Burpee is a full-body, multi-phase conditioning movement that chains a squat, a plank (with optional push-up), and an explosive vertical jump. The standard flow is: stand → squat down and place hands on the floor → jump or step the feet back to a plank (push-up top) → (optional push-up) → jump or step the feet forward → explosively jump up with the arms overhead → land and repeat. The canonical sub-poses are (a) the standing jump-top with arms overhead and (b) the bottom plank position (body straight, hands and toes on the floor, like the top of a push-up), plus the intermediate squat-thrust bottom (hands on floor, hips hinged, feet together). It is a cornerstone metabolic/calisthenic exercise.

## 2. Pose Purpose
- Develop full-body power, coordination, and conditioning in one cyclic pattern.
- Train the transition between squat, plank, and vertical jump under fatigue.
- Build hip/leg power (explosive extension) and trunk rigidity (plank).
- Improve work capacity and cardiovascular demand.
- Reinforce safe landing mechanics (soft knees, braced trunk).

## 3. Whole-Body Alignment
- The movement flows through distinct postures; alignment must hold in each:
  - **Stand:** tall, feet hip-width, trunk upright.
  - **Squat-bottom / hands-down:** hips hinge and knees flex, hands reach the floor.
  - **Plank:** straight line head-to-heels, hands and toes on floor.
  - **Jump-top:** full hip/knee/ankle extension, arms overhead, body tall.
- The trunk stays braced in every phase; the spine stays neutral (not rounded in the squat, not arched in the plank).
- The motion is continuous and coordinated.

## 4. Head
- Head is in neutral alignment with the spine in each phase.
- In the squat/hands-down the head leads the hinge (neutral, looking forward/down).
- In the plank the head is in line with the spine, eyes down.
- In the jump-top the head is tall, in line with the extended body, eyes forward.

## 5. Spine
- **Stand & jump-top:** neutral, tall, slight natural lordosis; the trunk is extended and braced.
- **Squat-bottom:** a hip hinge + knee flexion with the spine held neutral (chest up, back flat) — not rounded.
- **Plank:** neutral rigid line; no sag or arch.
- The spine does not flex/round at any transition; the core braces throughout.

## 6. Upper Body
- **Stand/jump:** arms drive overhead (shoulders flexed ~180°, palms together or apart) using the deltoids and upper trapezius/shoulder flexors.
- **Hands-down/plank:** hands under shoulders, arms extended (or in the optional push-up, elbows flex), shoulders depressed and stable.
- **Push-up (optional):** scapular protraction/retraction controlled; chest lowers toward the floor and presses back up.
- The shoulders bear load in the plank/push-up and drive in the jump.

## 7. Lower Body
- **Squat/land:** hips and knees flex (deep squat or partial), weight through the mid-foot/heel, knees tracking over the toes.
- **Plank:** feet together (or near), toes on floor, legs extended, glutes engaged.
- **Jump:** powerful triple extension — ankles plantar-flex, knees extend, hips extend — driven by the glutes, quadriceps, hamstrings, and calves.
- Landing: soft, with knees bent and absorbing, returning to the squat.
- Feet move together on the jump-back/forward transitions (or step for regressions).

## 8. Contacts
- **Stand/jump:** feet (whole foot or ball) on the floor.
- **Hands-down/squat-thrust:** hands (palms) on the floor, feet on the floor.
- **Plank/push-up:** hands (palms) on the floor under shoulders, toes on the floor.
- The jump-top has no hand contact; the landing returns foot contact.

## 9. Range of Motion
- Hip/knee/ankle: from deep flexion (squat/landing) to full extension (jump-top).
- Shoulder: from down (plank) to full flexion overhead (jump-top).
- Spine: neutral throughout; transitions move the whole body around the stable trunk.
- The cyclic range is large; tempo from controlled to fast.

## 10. Muscle Intent
- Legs (quadriceps, glutes, hamstrings, calves): the squat, the jump drive, and the landing absorb.
- Core (rectus abdominis, obliques, transversus, erector spinae): brace the trunk through every phase.
- Shoulders/deltoids, trapezius, triceps: support the plank/push-up and drive the arms overhead.
- Hip flexors: assist the foot-return to the hands.
- Calves: plantar-flex for the jump and absorb on landing.

## 11. Visual Checkpoints
- [ ] Standing phases show a tall, upright trunk with neutral spine.
- [ ] The squat/hands-down keeps the back flat (chest up), not rounded.
- [ ] The plank is a straight head-to-heel line, hands under shoulders.
- [ ] (Optional) push-up lowers and presses with a straight body.
- [ ] The jump-top achieves full hip/knee/ankle extension with arms overhead.
- [ ] The landing is soft with bent knees and a braced trunk.
- [ ] Hands plant firmly under the shoulders in the bottom.
- [ ] Feet move together on the transitions (or step cleanly).
- [ ] Knees track over the toes in the squat/landing.
- [ ] The head stays neutral in each phase.
- [ ] Shoulders are stable and down in the plank, not shrugged.
- [ ] The spine does not round during the squat or arch in the plank.
- [ ] Each phase flows without pausing in a collapsed position.
- [ ] Breathing is rhythmic (exhale on the jump/push, inhale on the return).
- [ ] The movement is symmetrical and coordinated.

## 12. Common Mistakes
- Rounding the low back in the squat/hands-down (loss of the flat-back hinge).
- Letting the plank sag or the hips hike.
- Not achieving full extension at the jump-top (cutting the rep short).
- Landing stiff-legged (no knee bend) — risky for the knees.
- Letting the knees cave inward.
- Shrugging the shoulders in the plank.
- Pausing in a collapsed squat between reps.

## 13. Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] The flow moves continuously through stand → hands-down → plank → (push-up) → return → jump-top with correct alignment in each phase.
- [ ] The spine remains neutral (flat-back squat, rigid plank, tall jump) throughout.
- [ ] The jump-top reaches full lower-body extension with arms overhead and the landing is soft and controlled.
- [ ] Hands plant under the shoulders and the feet transition together or step cleanly.
- [ ] The movement is coordinated, symmetrical, and breathing-paced.

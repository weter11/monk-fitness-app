# Push-Up (Diamond) — Biomechanical Pose Specification

## 1. Exercise Overview

- **Movement goal:** A prone, closed-chain horizontal press performed with the hands brought together under the sternum so the thumbs and index fingers touch, forming a diamond; the narrow base forces the elbows to tuck tightly to the ribs.
- **Training goal:** Maximize triceps brachii recruitment (particularly the medial head) and inner-pectoral fiber involvement while minimizing the leverage advantage of the chest; a high-demand close-grip press.
- **Intended muscles:** Triceps brachii (dominant, especially medial head), pectoralis major (inner/sternal fibers), anterior deltoid, serratus anterior, coracobrachialis, rectus abdominis, transverse abdominis, gluteus maximus, quadriceps.
- **Common execution style:** Hands placed together centered under the sternum (thumbs and index fingers touching to form a diamond); elbows pinned tightly to the torso; descent brings the chest toward the hands; strict, full-range, trunk rigid.

## 2. Pose Purpose

- **Why it exists:** The diamond (close-grip) push-up is the most triceps-biased push-up variant; the near-zero hand base and tightly tucked elbows place the elbow extensors at maximum mechanical disadvantage-for-leverage (maximum demand) and minimize pectoral contribution.
- **Canonical sub-poses:**
  - **Top:** arms extended, hands together under sternum, elbows tucked, body rigid.
  - **Bottom (depth):** elbows flexed (~90°) hugging the ribs, chest lowered toward the diamond hands.
  - **Transition:** descent/ascent with elbows glued to the torso.
- **Scapular strategy:** Subtle scapular protraction at bottom, retraction at top, depressed and flat; with the humerus adducted hard, scapular motion is minimal and winging must be firmly avoided.

## 3. Whole-Body Alignment

- **Center of mass:** Projected over the narrow central hand base and the toe contacts; tracks vertically.
- **Balance:** Stable, bilateral, symmetrical; the very narrow base demands high trunk control.
- **Symmetry:** Mirror-symmetric; equal load both hands/feet.
- **Posture:** Neutral spine; straight line acromion–greater trochanter–lateral malleolus.
- **Visual appearance:** A rigid plank with the hands fused into a single central diamond under the chest; the elbows hug the sides so the front silhouette is very slim; the chest passes close to the hands at the bottom.

## 4. Head

- **Neck:** Neutral, continuation of spine.
- **Gaze:** At floor just ahead of the hands.
- **Chin:** Slightly tucked.
- **Cervical alignment:** Smooth single curve.

## 5. Spine

- **Lumbar:** Neutral, braced.
- **Thoracic:** Neutral to slightly extended; flat.
- **Rib cage:** Closed/down.
- **Pelvis:** Neutral, level.
- **Summary state:** Neutral spine, isometric, unchanged top/bottom.

## 6. Upper Body

- **Shoulders (glenohumeral):** Humeri adducted and close to the torso; at the bottom the upper arms are near-parallel to the trunk (~0–20° from the ribs), producing the steepest press angle of any push-up variant.
- **Scapula:** Depressed, flat, minimal motion; no winging, no shrug.
- **Clavicles:** Level, symmetric.
- **Elbows:** Tucked tightly to the ribs; flexed to ~90° at bottom; at top extended. They must not flare outward.
- **Forearms:** Nearly vertical and close together (palms meet at the diamond); neutral rotation; wrists under/near the elbows.
- **Wrists:** Extended, straight; load through the palm base near the thumb–index diamond.
- **Hands:** Thumbs and index fingers touching under the sternum forming a diamond; fingers forward; pressure even across both hands (which act as one base).
- **Expected visual appearance:** Hands fused in a central diamond; elbows pinned to the sides; very narrow front silhouette; chest near the hands at bottom.

## 7. Lower Body

- **Hip:** Extended/neutral; extensors hold pelvis level.
- **Femur:** In line with trunk.
- **Knee:** Extended, straight.
- **Ankle:** Plantarflexed; balls of feet.
- **Foot:** Toe/ball contact.
- **Toe direction:** Pointed straight back.
- **Weight distribution:** Majority on hands; symmetric.

## 8. Contacts

- **Hand contact (single fused base):** Thumbs and index fingers touching, palms flat under the sternum; even pressure; the central pivot. Visually: a diamond of hands, wrists near elbows, forearms near-vertical.
- **Toe contact (×2):** Balls of feet; light; stable rear pivot. Visually: heels up, toes back.

## 9. Range of Motion

- **Expected ROM:** Elbow flexion top ~180° to bottom ~90°; humerus adducted ~0–20° from torso; chest descends to near the diamond hands.
- **Acceptable variation:** Chest within ~10 cm of (or touching near) the hands; elbow 70–100°; elbows remain tucked.
- **Unacceptable variation:** Elbows flaring outward (loses the close-grip intent); chest far from hands; pelvis sag; wrist pain from the narrow base.

## 10. Muscle Intent

- **Visibly engaged:** Triceps brachii (dominant, medial head firms hard), inner pectoralis major (subtle), anterior deltoid, serratus anterior (flat scapulae), abdominal wall, gluteals, quadriceps.

## 11. Visual Checkpoints

- [ ] Body one straight line shoulder–hip–ankle.
- [ ] Hands form a diamond under the sternum (thumbs + index touching).
- [ ] Elbows tucked tightly to ribs (0–20° from torso), not flared.
- [ ] Forearms near-vertical and close together.
- [ ] Head neutral, gaze at floor ahead.
- [ ] Neutral spine; pelvis level.
- [ ] Rib cage closed.
- [ ] Scapulae flat, depressed, no winging/shrug.
- [ ] Chest descends near the diamond hands at bottom.
- [ ] Elbow angle ~90° at bottom.
- [ ] Knees straight, weight on balls of feet.
- [ ] Feet symmetric, toes back.
- [ ] Symmetric load left/right.
- [ ] No torso rotation.
- [ ] Trunk rigid across full ROM.
- [ ] Movement controlled both directions.
- [ ] Hips not dropped below shoulder line.
- [ ] Neck long.
- [ ] Wrists straight.

## 12. Common Mistakes

- **Elbows flaring:** The defining error — elbows splay outward, destroying the close-grip/triceps bias and turning it into a standard push-up. Visually the upper arms open away from the torso. Wrong because it defeats the variant's purpose.
- **Sagging hips:** Pelvis drops, lumbar rounds. Wrong — lumbar load.
- **Half reps:** Chest stays high above the hands. Wrong — fails ROM.
- **Butt-up:** Inverted-V at hips. Wrong — shortens lever.
- **Wrist strain from narrow base:** Hands too close causing painful wrist extension. Wrong — joint stress; keep the diamond just under the sternum.
- **Winging scapulae:** Medial borders lift. Wrong — unstable shoulder.

## 13. Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Straight-line plank maintained.
- [ ] Hands form a diamond under sternum; elbows tucked to ribs throughout.
- [ ] Forearms near-vertical; wrists straight.
- [ ] Chest descends to within ~10 cm of the hands (~90° elbow).
- [ ] Neutral spine/pelvis; no sag, no butt-up.
- [ ] Knees straight, weight on balls of feet.
- [ ] Symmetric, no rotation.
- [ ] Scapulae flat/depressed, no winging/shrug.
- [ ] Full, controlled ROM both directions.

# Push-Up (Knee) — Biomechanical Pose Specification

## 1. Exercise Overview

- **Movement goal:** A regressed prone, closed-chain horizontal press performed on the knees rather than the toes; the body pivots at the knee joint, shortening the lever and reducing load while preserving the pressing pattern.
- **Training goal:** Build foundational pushing strength and trunk bracing with lower demand than the standard push-up; a common regression for beginners, rehab, or higher-rep endurance.
- **Intended muscles:** Pectoralis major, anterior deltoid, triceps brachii, serratus anterior, rectus abdominis, transverse abdominis, gluteus maximus (hip extension hold), quadriceps (knee support).
- **Common execution style:** From a kneeling plank, hands placed under the shoulders, the body held straight from the shoulders through the hips to the KNEES (the lower legs fold back behind), the descent driven by elbow flexion, the ascent by extension; the pivot is the knee/lower-leg contact, not the ankle.

## 2. Pose Purpose

- **Why it exists:** The knee push-up is a load-reducing regression of the standard push-up: shortening the body lever about the knee reduces the moment arm at the shoulder, making the press accessible while training the same movement pattern and trunk rigidity.
- **Canonical sub-poses:**
  - **Top (kneeling plank):** arms extended, body straight shoulder→hip→knee, lower legs folded back.
  - **Bottom (depth):** elbows flexed, chest lowered toward floor, trunk rigid.
  - **Transition:** descent/ascent with trunk shape unchanged.
- **Scapular strategy:** Same as standard — gentle protraction at bottom, retraction at top, depressed, flat, no winging.

## 3. Whole-Body Alignment

- **Center of mass:** Projected over the hand base and the knee/lower-leg contact; tracks vertically during the movement.
- **Balance:** Stable, bilateral, symmetrical on the knees.
- **Symmetry:** Mirror-symmetric; equal load both hands and both knees.
- **Posture:** Neutral spine; the rigid line runs from acromion through greater trochanter to the knee (NOT to the ankle — the shins fold back behind).
- **Visual appearance:** A shortened plank hinged at the knees; from the side the body is straight from shoulders to knees, then the lower legs angle back to the floor; the hip is the highest rear point of the rigid segment.

## 4. Head

- **Neck:** Neutral, continuation of spine.
- **Gaze:** At floor ahead of hands.
- **Chin:** Slightly tucked.
- **Cervical alignment:** Smooth single curve.

## 5. Spine

- **Lumbar:** Neutral, braced.
- **Thoracic:** Neutral to slightly extended; flat.
- **Rib cage:** Closed/down.
- **Pelvis:** Neutral, level — the pelvis stays in line with the shoulder–knee line; no anterior or posterior tilt.
- **Summary state:** Neutral spine, isometric, unchanged top/bottom.

## 6. Upper Body

- **Shoulders (glenohumeral):** Humeri abducted ~45–70° from torso at the bottom; the shoulders are the pivot for the upper body.
- **Scapula:** Depressed, flat, protract at bottom/retract at top; no winging.
- **Clavicles:** Level, symmetric.
- **Elbows:** Flexed to ~90° at bottom; track ~45–60° from torso; at top extended.
- **Forearms:** Vertical, wrists under elbows.
- **Wrists:** Extended, straight; load through palm.
- **Hands:** Flat, under shoulders; fingers forward.
- **Expected visual appearance:** Hands under shoulders; elbows moderately flared; shoulders broad and stable.

## 7. Lower Body

- **Hip:** Extended/neutral; hip extensors hold the pelvis aligned with the shoulder–knee line.
- **Femur:** In line with the trunk from hip to knee (thighs angled down to the floor at the knee).
- **Knee:** The pivot/contact joint; the knee is flexed ~90° so the lower legs fold back; the thigh is the rigid segment, the shins lie back along the floor.
- **Ankle:** Relaxed in a dorsiflexed/neutral position as the lower legs rest on the floor; not bearing primary load.
- **Foot:** The dorsal (top) surface of the feet and/or the knees contact; toes may be pointed or flexed naturally; the feet lie back behind the knees.
- **Toe direction:** N/A as primary — feet trail behind; if toes tucked, they point back.
- **Weight distribution:** Majority on the hands; remainder on the knees/lower legs; symmetric.

## 8. Contacts

- **Hand contact (×2):** Flat palms/fingers under shoulders; even pressure; primary pivot. Visually: hands flat, wrists under elbows.
- **Knee / lower-leg contact (×2):** The front of the knees (and often the shins/instep) rest on the floor; stable rear pivot. Visually: knees on floor, lower legs folded back, feet trailing.

## 9. Range of Motion

- **Expected ROM:** Elbow flexion top ~180° to bottom ~90°; chest descends toward floor; the trunk rotates about the knee so the shoulder drops toward the hands.
- **Acceptable variation:** Chest within ~10 cm of floor; elbow 70–100°; trunk straight shoulder→knee.
- **Unacceptable variation:** Hips rising above the shoulder–knee line (butt-up); pelvis sagging below it (lumbar round); chest far from floor; knees splayed unevenly.

## 10. Muscle Intent

- **Visibly engaged:** Pectoralis major, anterior deltoid, triceps, serratus anterior (flat scapulae), abdominal wall (braced, no sag at hip), gluteus maximus (pelvis level), quadriceps (knee support).

## 11. Visual Checkpoints

- [ ] Body straight from shoulders through hips to KNEES (not to ankles).
- [ ] Lower legs folded back behind the knees.
- [ ] Hands under shoulders.
- [ ] Head neutral, gaze at floor ahead.
- [ ] Neutral spine; pelvis level (no butt-up, no sag).
- [ ] Rib cage closed.
- [ ] Scapulae flat, depressed, no winging.
- [ ] Elbows track ~45–60° from torso.
- [ ] Chest descends near floor at bottom.
- [ ] Elbow angle ~90° at bottom.
- [ ] Knees on floor, symmetric.
- [ ] Symmetric load left/right.
- [ ] No torso rotation.
- [ ] Trunk rigid across full ROM.
- [ ] Movement controlled both directions.
- [ ] Hips not above the shoulder–knee line.
- [ ] Neck long.
- [ ] Wrists straight under elbows.

## 12. Common Mistakes

- **Butt-up (hips above shoulder–knee line):** The pelvis rises; visually the rear arcs upward. Wrong because it shortens the lever and removes trunk work, and breaks the straight shoulder–knee line.
- **Sagging hips (lumbar round):** Pelvis drops below the line; visually a dip at the waist. Wrong — lumbar load.
- **Half reps:** Chest stays high. Wrong — fails ROM.
- **Knees uneven / splayed:** One knee off the floor or knees wide; visually asymmetric. Wrong — unstable, asymmetric load.
- **Winging scapulae:** Medial borders lift. Wrong — unstable shoulder.
- **Shrugging:** Shoulders to ears. Wrong — neck strain.

## 13. Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Straight line shoulder–hip–knee maintained; lower legs folded back.
- [ ] Hands under shoulders; elbows track ~45–60° from torso.
- [ ] Chest descends to within ~10 cm of floor (~90° elbow).
- [ ] Neutral spine/pelvis; no butt-up, no sag.
- [ ] Knees on floor, symmetric.
- [ ] No torso rotation; balanced load.
- [ ] Scapulae flat/depressed, no winging/shrug.
- [ ] Full, controlled ROM both directions.

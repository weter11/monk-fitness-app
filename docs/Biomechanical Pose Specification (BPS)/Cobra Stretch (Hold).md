# Cobra Stretch (Hold)

## 1. Exercise Overview
The Cobra Stretch is a prone (face-down) spinal extension hold drawn from yoga and mobility practice. Lying on the stomach, the hands are placed under the shoulders and the chest is pressed upward as the spine extends into a gentle backbend. The hips and legs remain on the floor. The canonical held position is the upward chest-lift with the low back slightly arched and the anterior chain (abdominals, hip flexors) lengthened. It is a static extension stretch for the front of the body and a gentle mobilization of the thoracic/lumbar spine.

## 2. Pose Purpose
- Lengthen the anterior chain: abdominal muscles and hip flexors (rectus abdominis, psoas).
- Mobilize the thoracic and lumbar spine into extension (counter to flexion-dominated postures).
- Open the chest and improve pectoral/shoulder flexion range.
- Provide a gentle, supported backbend appropriate for beginners.
- Improve spinal segmental awareness in extension.

## 3. Whole-Body Alignment
- Prone: stomach and legs on the floor, pelvis grounded.
- Hands under the shoulders, elbows close to the body.
- The chest lifts by extending the spine; the pelvis and thighs stay down.
- The backbend is gentle — the low back is not compressed, the extension is distributed through the thorax and lumbar.
- Shoulders are down and back, not shrugged toward the ears.

## 4. Head
- Head is in line with the extended spine, looking slightly forward/up (not cranked back).
- The cervical spine extends gently as a continuation of the thoracic curve.
- The chin is not jutting; the neck is long.

## 5. Spine
- **Thoracic + lumbar:** extended into a gentle backbend; the upper back does most of the visible lifting.
- The lumbar contribution should be moderate — avoid jamming the low back by pushing the hips up or over-arching.
- **Cervical:** neutral-to-slightly extended, following the thorax.
- The extension is smooth along the spine, not a hinge at one segment.

## 6. Upper Body
- Hands press down/into the floor under the shoulders to assist the lift, but the primary movement is spinal extension (not pushing up with straight arms into a full updog).
- Shoulders are depressed and retracted (down and slightly back), not shrugged.
- Elbows stay close to the ribs.
- The chest opens; the pectorals lengthen.
- Arms may be bent (gentle cobra) or straighter (deeper) depending on mobility — consensus: keep elbows bent for a true "cobra" stretch rather than a full upward dog.

## 7. Lower Body
- Pelvis, thighs, and legs remain flat on the floor.
- Knees may be extended (legs straight, tops of feet on floor) or bent with feet up — straight-leg is canonical for the stretch.
- Glutes are soft (not clenched) to avoid over-compressing the low back; a light engagement is acceptable to protect the lumbar.
- Hips stay grounded; they do not lift.

## 8. Contacts
- Abdomen/pelvis and the anterior thighs/legs contact the floor.
- Hands (palms) press into the floor under the shoulders.
- Tops of the feet (or toes if feet flexed) contact the floor.

## 9. Range of Motion
- Spinal extension: from prone neutral to a mild-to-moderate backbend (thoracic + lumbar).
- Shoulder flexion: arms assist to ~90° or more as the chest lifts.
- Hip extension (anterior): the hip flexors lengthen as the pelvis stays grounded.
- Held 15–60 s; the depth is limited by comfort in the low back and front of the hips.

## 10. Muscle Intent
- Erector spinae and multifidus: concentrically lift the spine into extension.
- Hip flexors (iliopsoas) and rectus abdominis: lengthened (stretched) on the anterior side.
- Pectorals: lengthened as the chest opens.
- Lower trapezius / serratus: depress the shoulders.
- Glutes: lightly engaged (or relaxed) to protect the lumbar without jamming.

## 11. Visual Checkpoints
- [ ] Body is prone, pelvis and legs flat on the floor.
- [ ] Hands are under the shoulders, elbows close to the ribs.
- [ ] The chest lifts via spinal extension, not just arm pushing.
- [ ] Shoulders are down and back, not shrugged toward the ears.
- [ ] The low back is in a gentle arch, not jammed or over-arched.
- [ ] The pelvis and thighs stay grounded (hips do not lift).
- [ ] Head is neutral, looking forward, not cranked back.
- [ ] The anterior chain (belly, front of hips) feels lengthened.
- [ ] Neck is long, chin not jutting.
- [ ] Breathing is smooth; the chest expands.
- [ ] The backbend is distributed along the spine, not hinged at one point.
- [ ] Glutes are not aggressively clenching.
- [ ] The position is held statically, not bouncing.
- [ ] Knees are extended (legs straight) for the canonical stretch.
- [ ] No sharp low-back pain.

## 12. Common Mistakes
- Shrugging the shoulders up toward the ears.
- Lifting the hips/pelvis off the floor (turning it into upward dog).
- Over-arching the low back by pushing too hard with straight arms.
- Cranking the neck back instead of extending the whole spine.
- Clenching the glutes so the low back compresses.
- Holding the breath.
- Letting the elbows wing far out from the ribs.

## 13. Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] The spine extends into a gentle backbend with the chest lifted and the pelvis grounded.
- [ ] Shoulders are depressed and retracted, not elevated.
- [ ] The anterior chain (abdominals, hip flexors) is lengthened without low-back compression.
- [ ] The head and neck follow the spinal extension in neutral alignment.
- [ ] The position is held statically with relaxed breathing and no sharp low-back pain.

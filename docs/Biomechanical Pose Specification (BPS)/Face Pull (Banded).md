# Face Pull (Banded)

## 1. Exercise Overview
The Face Pull is a standing pulling exercise typically performed with a band (or cable) anchored in front of the athlete at roughly head height. The athlete pulls the band toward the face with the elbows high and wide, finishing with external rotation of the shoulders so the fists rotate toward the forehead/temples. It is a primary exercise for shoulder health, training scapular retraction and posterior-shoulder (rear deltoid, rotator cuff) strength, and counteracting rounded-shoulder posture. The canonical sub-pose is the pulled-in end position: elbows bent and wide, hands near the temples, forearms vertical, the band drawn to the face.

## 2. Pose Purpose
- Strengthen the rear deltoids, rotator cuff, and scapular retractors (trapezius, rhomboids).
- Train shoulder external rotation under load — protective for the glenohumeral joint.
- Improve scapular retraction and depression (pulling the shoulder blades together and down).
- Counteract internal-rotation/rounded-shoulder posture from pressing and desk work.
- Provide a low-risk shoulder-health and posture drill.

## 3. Whole-Body Alignment
- Standing tall, feet roughly hip-width, knees soft, trunk upright and braced.
- The band anchor is at or slightly above head height in front of the athlete.
- The pull is horizontal (toward the face); the body does not lean back to cheat the movement.
- The trunk stays still; the motion is at the shoulders and scapulae.
- A slight step back to tension the band is normal; the stance is stable.

## 4. Head
- Head is in neutral, upright alignment — not jutting forward to meet the band.
- The cervical spine is tall; the chin is not poked forward.
- The face receives the band at eye/temple level without the head dropping or craning.

## 5. Spine
- **Whole spine:** neutral and upright (natural lordosis/thoracic curve), braced lightly.
- There is no spinal flexion or rotation; the trunk is a stable base.
- The thoracic stays long and slightly extended (good posture), not rounded forward.

## 6. Upper Body
- **Start:** arms extended forward, hands holding the band, shoulders in slight external rotation.
- **Pull (canonical end):** elbows bend and drive outward/backward (high and wide), the band comes to the face, and the shoulders rotate externally so the fists finish near the temples/forehead with forearms vertical.
- Scapulae retract (squeeze together) and depress (down) at the end.
- The rear deltoids and rotator cuff do the work; the chest stays open.
- Avoid shrugging (upper traps) — the pull is from the mid/lower traps and rear delts.

## 7. Lower Body
- Feet hip-width, knees soft (slightly bent), weight evenly distributed.
- The legs are stable and quiet; they do not drive the pull.
- Hips neutral; the pelvis does not shift or rotate.
- The lower body is simply a grounded base.

## 8. Contacts
- Feet on the floor (stable stance).
- Hands grip the band (or rope/cable attachment).
- The band/cable is anchored to an external point in front at head height — a prop, not a body contact.
- No other body contact; the movement is freestanding.

## 9. Range of Motion
- Shoulder horizontal abduction / transverse extension: from forward (arms extended) to the band at the face with elbows wide.
- Elbow flexion: to ~90° at the end.
- Shoulder external rotation: peaks at the end as the fists rotate outward toward the temples.
- Scapular retraction/depression: end-range pinch-and-down.
- Typically 2–3 sets of 12–20 controlled reps.

## 10. Muscle Intent
- Rear deltoid (posterior deltoid): primary mover of the horizontal pull.
- Rotator cuff (infraspinatus, teres minor) and posterior capsule: control and finish external rotation.
- Middle/lower trapezius and rhomboids: retract and depress the scapulae.
- Biceps/brachialis: assist elbow flexion.
- Core and spinal erectors: keep the trunk upright and still.

## 11. Visual Checkpoints
- [ ] Stance is tall and stable, trunk upright and braced.
- [ ] The band anchor is at roughly head height in front.
- [ ] Arms start extended toward the band.
- [ ] At the end the elbows are high and wide (bent ~90°).
- [ ] The band comes to the face, fists near the temples/forehead.
- [ ] The shoulders finish in external rotation (thumbs/backs of hands rotate outward).
- [ ] The shoulder blades squeeze together and down (not shrugged up).
- [ ] The chest is open, not collapsed forward.
- [ ] The head stays neutral, not jutting forward.
- [ ] The trunk does not lean back to cheat the pull.
- [ ] The neck is long, no upper-trap shrugging.
- [ ] Both arms move symmetrically.
- [ ] The movement is controlled, not snapped.
- [ ] Knees stay soft, weight even.
- [ ] Breathing is smooth (exhale on the pull).

## 12. Common Mistakes
- Shrugging the shoulders up (overusing upper traps) instead of retracting/depressing.
- Letting the head jut forward to meet the band.
- Leaning the whole body back to pull the band.
- Not finishing external rotation (just a row to the chest, not to the face).
- Allowing the elbows to drop low (losing the "high and wide" scapular pattern).
- Rounding the upper back forward.
- Using momentum/snapping instead of control.

## 13. Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] The athlete pulls the band to the face with elbows high and wide and finishes in shoulder external rotation (fists near temples).
- [ ] The scapulae retract and depress with the chest open and head neutral.
- [ ] The trunk stays upright and still; the body does not lean back.
- [ ] Both arms move symmetrically with controlled tempo and breathing.
- [ ] No upper-trap shrugging or head-jut compensations are present.

# Push-Up (Military) — Biomechanical Pose Specification

## 1. Exercise Overview

- **Movement goal:** A prone, closed-chain horizontal press performed with the hands placed narrow and directly under the shoulders, the forearms vertical and parallel (neutral), and the elbows tucked tightly to the ribs — the strict "front leaning rest" alignment used in military physical training.
- **Training goal:** Maximize triceps brachii (especially the long and medial heads) and anterior deltoid recruitment while minimizing pectoral leverage; build strict pressing discipline and trunk rigidity.
- **Intended muscles:** Triceps brachii (dominant), anterior deltoid, pectoralis major (lesser, inner fibers), serratus anterior, coracobrachialis, rectus abdominis, transverse abdominis, gluteus maximus, quadriceps.
- **Common execution style:** Hands placed roughly shoulder-width or slightly narrower, palms facing forward (or neutral), directly beneath the shoulders; elbows pinned close to the torso (tracking ~10–30° from the ribs) throughout the descent so the upper arms stay near-parallel to the trunk; strict, slow, full-range reps with an immovable trunk.

## 2. Pose Purpose

- **Why it exists:** The military (close, strict) push-up is the standardized pressing test of upper-body pushing endurance; the narrow, tucked-elbow position maximizes triceps and front-deltoid work and removes the pectoral leverage advantage of wider stances.
- **Canonical sub-poses:**
  - **Top (front leaning rest):** arms extended, hands under shoulders, elbows tucked, body rigid.
  - **Bottom (depth):** elbows flexed to ~90°, upper arms hugging the ribs, chest lowered toward the floor.
  - **Transition:** descent/ascent with elbows staying glued to the torso.
- **Scapular strategy:** Scapulae protract slightly at the bottom, retract at the top, kept depressed and flat (no winging, no shrug); because the humerus is adducted close to the torso, scapular motion is subtler than in the wide variant.

## 3. Whole-Body Alignment

- **Center of mass:** Projected vertically over the narrow hand base and the toe contacts; tracks over the hand line.
- **Balance:** Stable, bilateral, symmetrical; the narrower base demands more trunk control.
- **Symmetry:** Mirror-symmetric; equal load both hands/feet.
- **Posture:** Neutral spine; straight line acromion–greater trochanter–lateral malleolus.
- **Visual appearance:** A rigid plank with a narrow, "ramrod" hand stance; the elbows hug the ribs so the silhouette from the front is slim; strict, upright-pressed look.

## 4. Head

- **Neck:** Neutral, continuation of the spine.
- **Gaze:** At the floor just ahead of the hands.
- **Chin:** Slightly tucked.
- **Cervical alignment:** Smooth single curve.

## 5. Spine

- **Lumbar:** Neutral, braced.
- **Thoracic:** Neutral to slightly extended; flat upper back.
- **Rib cage:** Closed/down.
- **Pelvis:** Neutral, level.
- **Summary state:** Neutral spine, isometric, unchanged top to bottom.

## 6. Upper Body

- **Shoulders (glenohumeral):** Humeri close to the torso; at the bottom the upper arms are roughly 10–30° from the trunk (near-parallel), producing a steep press angle that biases triceps and front deltoid.
- **Scapula:** Depressed, flat, gently protracting at bottom; no winging, no shrug.
- **Clavicles:** Level, symmetric.
- **Elbows:** Tucked tightly to the ribs; flexed to ~90° at bottom; at top extended (not aggressively locked). The elbows should not flare outward.
- **Forearms:** Vertical and parallel (neutral rotation), palms forward or neutral, wrists under elbows.
- **Wrists:** Extended, straight; load through palm.
- **Hands:** Flat, placed directly under the shoulders (shoulder-width or slightly narrower), fingers forward.
- **Expected visual appearance:** Narrow hand base; elbows pinned to the sides; slim front silhouette; shoulders directly above the hands.

## 7. Lower Body

- **Hip:** Extended/neutral; extensors hold pelvis level.
- **Femur:** In line with trunk.
- **Knee:** Extended, straight.
- **Ankle:** Plantarflexed; balls of feet.
- **Foot:** Toe/ball contact.
- **Toe direction:** Pointed straight back.
- **Weight distribution:** Majority on hands; symmetric.

## 8. Contacts

- **Hand contact (×2):** Flat palms/fingers directly under shoulders; even pressure; narrow stable pivot. Visually: hands close, wrists under elbows, forearms vertical.
- **Toe contact (×2):** Balls of feet; light; stable rear pivot. Visually: heels up, toes back.

## 9. Range of Motion

- **Expected ROM:** Elbow flexion top ~180° to bottom ~90°; humerus adducted ~10–30° from torso at bottom; chest to within ~10 cm of floor.
- **Acceptable variation:** Chest within ~10 cm of floor; elbow 70–100°; elbows remain tucked.
- **Unacceptable variation:** Elbows flaring out (loses the strict tuck and becomes a standard/wide push-up); chest far from floor; pelvis sag.

## 10. Muscle Intent

- **Visibly engaged:** Triceps brachii (dominant, back of arm firms hard on extension), anterior deltoid (front shoulder), inner pectoralis (subtle), serratus anterior (flat scapulae), abdominal wall, gluteals, quadriceps (rigid lower body).

## 11. Visual Checkpoints

- [ ] Body one straight line shoulder–hip–ankle.
- [ ] Hands directly under shoulders (shoulder-width or slightly narrower).
- [ ] Elbows tucked to ribs (10–30° from torso), not flared.
- [ ] Forearms vertical and parallel, palms forward/neutral.
- [ ] Head neutral, gaze at floor ahead.
- [ ] Neutral spine; pelvis level.
- [ ] Rib cage closed.
- [ ] Scapulae flat, depressed, no winging/shrug.
- [ ] Chest descends near floor at bottom.
- [ ] Elbow angle ~90° at bottom.
- [ ] Knees straight, weight on balls of feet.
- [ ] Feet symmetric, toes back.
- [ ] Symmetric load left/right.
- [ ] No torso rotation.
- [ ] Trunk rigid across full ROM.
- [ ] Movement controlled both directions.
- [ ] Hips not dropped below shoulder line.
- [ ] Neck long.
- [ ] Strict, "front leaning rest" alignment.

## 12. Common Mistakes

- **Elbows flaring:** The defining error — elbows leave the ribs and splay outward, turning the move into a standard/wide push-up and shifting load off the triceps. Visually the upper arms open away from the torso. Wrong because it defeats the variation's purpose and stresses the shoulder.
- **Sagging hips:** Pelvis drops, lumbar rounds. Wrong — lumbar load.
- **Half reps:** Chest stays high. Wrong — fails ROM.
- **Butt-up:** Inverted-V at hips. Wrong — shortens lever.
- **Winging scapulae:** Medial borders lift. Wrong — unstable shoulder.
- **Shrugging:** Shoulders to ears. Wrong — neck strain.

## 13. Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Straight-line plank maintained.
- [ ] Hands directly under shoulders; elbows tucked to ribs throughout.
- [ ] Forearms vertical/parallel, palms forward/neutral.
- [ ] Chest descends to within ~10 cm of floor (~90° elbow).
- [ ] Neutral spine/pelvis; no sag, no butt-up.
- [ ] Knees straight, weight on balls of feet.
- [ ] Symmetric, no rotation.
- [ ] Scapulae flat/depressed, no winging/shrug.
- [ ] Full, controlled ROM both directions.
